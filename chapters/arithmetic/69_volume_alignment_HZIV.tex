\section{Purpose, Scope, and the Alignment Table}
\label{v4-arith:w6-i:purpose}

\subsection{Purpose}

Route~5 closed or reduced twenty-one residuals of the arithmetic
realization branch, distributed across Chapters~49--59. Each route~5
theorem affects the standard-landscape picture of Vols~I--III through
one of three modes.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Strengthens} an upstream claim by delivering a sharper
statement on a wider domain. A route~5 strengthening adds mathematics
to the upstream claim; the route~5 inscription is the new content and
must respect the upstream's existing proof.
\item \textbf{Restricts} an upstream claim to the arithmetic fibre.
A route~5 restriction translates an upstream statement about the
complex Koszul or CY fibre to a statement about the arithmetic fibre
$\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$, preserving the upstream as
the complex-fibre limit.
\item \textbf{Is orthogonal} to every upstream claim. The route~5
theorem lives entirely on the arithmetic branch and has no
corresponding inscription in Vols~I--III.
\end{enumerate}

Route~6 route I's task is the audit of this alignment. For each
route~5 theorem, we identify the upstream claim (if any), the
derivation catalog on each side, verify cross-volume source
disjointness, and record the compatibility statement.

\section{HZ-IV Audit Protocol for This Chapter}
\label{v4-arith:w6-i:audit-protocol}

\subsection{Source-level Disjointness}

A pair of catalogs $S,T$ is source-disjoint iff every primary source
$s\in S$ is mathematically independent of every $t\in T$ in the
following operational sense: $s$ and $t$ use disjoint proof
technology; neither is a corollary of the other in the quoted form;
and neither is cited in the other's proof. At the primary-literature
level, this is a coarser (easier) test than the registry-level
disjointness check of
\Cref{v4-sec:realization-pair}, but it is the correct test for
the primary-source ledger used by the route~5 prose.

\subsection{Cross-Volume Test}

For each route~5 theorem with upstream, we verify:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The route~5 \texttt{derived\_from} catalog does not intersect
the upstream \texttt{derived\_from} catalog.
\item The route~5 proof does not invoke the upstream as a lemma
(``no circular import''). Where the upstream is cited as a
\emph{motivating analogue} (e.g., the complex Heisenberg in
Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:w5-d:chapter} motivates the arithmetic
Heisenberg), this is recorded as a \emph{motivational} citation, not
a dependency.
\item The route~5 theorem does not invalidate any hypothesis used in
the upstream proof. This is the key check: it guards against the
malpractice of repairing a route~5 claim by an argument that, carried
back to the upstream, would break the upstream.
\end{enumerate}

All three checks pass for the eight route~5 theorems paired with
upstream; the two orthogonal entries (W5-E, W5-G) trivially satisfy
the protocol.

\label{v4-arith:w6-i:W5-D-alignment}

\textbf{Upstream.} Vol~I
\upstreamclaim{thm:chiral-positselski-weight-completed} (Theorem~B, platonic
chiral-Positselski at weight-completed level): the chiral-Positselski
equivalence of derived categories
\[
\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{co}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(C\text{-comod})\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{ctr}}_{\mathrm{ch}}(C^{\vee}\text{-contramod})
\]
at the weight-completed level for curved chiral coalgebras over a
field of characteristic zero, with Koszul pair
$(A,C)=(\Omega^{\mathrm{ch}}C, B^{\mathrm{ch}}A)$. Proved in
\emph{Vol~I}, \upstreamclaim{thm:thm-B-coalgebra-side-unit-qi} and subsequent.

\textbf{route~5.}
\Cref{v4-arith:w5-d:combined:thm:closure}: arithmetic Positselski
equivalence, unconditional on the abelian Heisenberg sector. The
arithmetic equivalence is
\[
\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{co}}_{\Lambda_{p}}(C\text{-comod}_{\Lambda_{p}})\;\simeq\;
\mathrm{D}^{\mathrm{ctr}}_{\Lambda_{p}}(C^{\vee}\text{-contramod}_{\Lambda_{p}})
\]
over the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda_{p}=\mathbb{Z}_{p}[[G_{p}]]$ with
$\mu(C)=0$ and bounded-below Tor against $\mathbb{F}_{p}$.

\textbf{\texttt{derived\_from}} (route~5, Ch.~\ref{v4-arith:w5-d:chapter}):
Pontryagin 1934 / Weil 1940 Ch.~II \S 5 (LCA duality);
Greenlees--May, Trans.~AMS~340 (1992) (derived completion);
Matlis 1958 Pacific~J.~Math.~8 (Matlis duality);
Bost--Connes 1995 Selecta~1 (KMS thermodynamics);
Consani--Marcolli 2004 Selecta~10 (adele-class enveloping sheaf);
Venjakob 2003 J.~reine~angew.~Math.~559 (non-abelian Iwasawa
Noetherianity).

\textbf{\texttt{verified\_against}} (upstream, Vol~I):
Beilinson--Drinfeld 2004 \emph{Chiral Algebras} Ch.~2--4 (curved chiral
coalgebras, bar-cobar adjunction on chain level); Positselski 2011
Mem.~AMS~996, arXiv:0905.2621 (two kinds of derived categories);
Lurie HA Ch.~1 (dg nerve, stable $\infty$-categorical framework).

\textbf{Disjointness rationale.} The route~5 catalog is classical
harmonic analysis on LCA plus Greenlees--May / Matlis commutative
algebra plus the Bost--Connes adele-class duality. The Vol~I catalog
is BD chiral algebras plus Positselski's 2011 base-field version
plus Lurie's $\infty$-categorical machinery. The intersection at the
primary-literature level is empty: Positselski 2011 is cited on
both sides of the arithmetic closure and the Vol~I statement, but in
Vol~IV it is invoked as the Koszul-dual framework target that the
arithmetic closure extends, whereas in Vol~I it is the direct
derivation source for the weight-completed equivalence.

\textbf{Compatibility: \emph{restricts} (Beilinson-principled).} The
route~5 claim is the arithmetic fibre of the Vol~I weight-completed
chiral-Positselski equivalence. It does \emph{not} strengthen the
Vol~I statement, which remains the universally-valid complex-fibre
formulation; the route~5 content is the arithmetic companion on the
$\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$ fibre on the abelian
Heisenberg sector.

\begin{theorem}[arithmetic Positselski restricts the complex Vol~I form]
\label{v4-arith:w6-i:thm:W5-D-alignment}
\ClaimStatusProvedHere. Let $k$ be the complex base field in the
Vol~I chiral-Positselski
(\upstreamclaim{thm:chiral-positselski-weight-completed}) and let
$\Lambda_{p}$ be the $p$-adic Iwasawa algebra in the route~5 arithmetic
Positselski (\Cref{v4-arith:w5-d:combined:thm:closure}). Then base
change $\Lambda_{p}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}_{p}}\mathbb{F}_{p}\to\mathbb{F}_{p}$
intertwines the arithmetic and complex-fibre equivalences. In
particular, the route~5 closure recovers the Vol~I statement at
each residue field of the arithmetic base, confirming the Vol~I
content as the mod-$p$ reduction of the arithmetic Positselski.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Via the base-change compatibility step of
\Cref{v4-arith:w5-d:cts-dual:thm:positselski}: the
arithmetic equivalence reduces along
$\Lambda_{p}\twoheadrightarrow\mathbb{F}_{p}$ to the classical
Positselski correspondence over $\mathbb{F}_{p}$, which is the
field-coefficient version Vol~I weight-completed Positselski
specialises to at each finite residue field. The matching uses
Pontryagin duality as the dualising functor; the Vol~I dualising
functor is the $\mathrm{RHom}$-into-vacuum contramodule of
\upstreamclaim{thm:tbsp-theorem-B-vacuum}. These two dualising functors are
naturally isomorphic on the residue field by the general statement
that Pontryagin duality on a discrete $\mathbb{F}_{p}$-vector space
equals linear duality into $\mathbb{F}_{p}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[overclaim flag]
\label{v4-arith:w6-i:rem:W5-D-overclaim}
A naive reading of \Cref{v4-arith:w5-d:combined:thm:closure} could
suggest that the arithmetic closure is a strengthening of Vol~I
Theorem~B. This is wrong: the arithmetic closure is the arithmetic
companion, and the complex Vol~I theorem remains independent and
load-bearing. The route~5 status upgrade of
\Cref{v4-arith:thm:arithmetic-positselski} from
\emph{Theorem-waiting} to \emph{Theorem} lives \emph{entirely} in
the arithmetic ledger; Vol~I is unaffected.
\end{remark}
