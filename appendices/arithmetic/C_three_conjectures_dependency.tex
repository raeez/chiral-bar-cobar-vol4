\chapter{Dependency Graph of the Three Closing Conjectures}
\label{v4-arith:app:three-conjectures-dep}

The three conjectures F1, F2, F3 of Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:open-frontiers}
are hierarchically ordered and mutually consistent. This appendix
records the dependency graph explicitly, the local theorems each
imports, and the global gap each fills.

\section{The Dependency Graph}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:graph}

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzcd}[row sep=large, column sep=large]
\text{F1: global arith.~AG} \ar[r] \ar[d] & \text{F2: perfectoid }E_{2} \ar[d] \\
\text{Local F1 (Fargues--Scholze)} \ar[ur, dashed] & \text{F3: arith.~Ben-Zvi--Nadler}
\end{tikzcd}
\end{center}

F1 and F2 are each theorem-locally-conjecture-globally. F3 combines
the local theorems of F1 and F2 with arithmetic Ben-Zvi--Nadler
machinery to produce a global identification of the Hochschild
trace with $\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{prim}}$.

\section{F1: Global Arithmetic Arinkin--Gaitsgory}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F1-details}

\begin{conjecture}[restatement of F1]
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F1-restatement}
For $G=GL_{2}$ and the global Emerton--Gee stack
$\mathcal{X}^{\mathrm{EG,glob}}_{GL_{2}}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{D}([GL_{2}])^{\mathrm{sph\text{-}fin}}\;\simeq\;\mathrm{IndCoh}_{\mathrm{Nilp}}\bigl(\mathcal{X}^{\mathrm{EG,glob}}_{GL_{2}}\bigr).
\end{equation}
\end{conjecture}

\paragraph{Local ingredients (theorem).}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Fargues--Scholze arXiv:2102.13459 (local geometrization).
\item Caraiani--Scholze arXiv:1710.10543 (unitary Shimura variety case).
\item Emerton--Gee stack construction arXiv:2012.12403.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Global gap (conjecture).}
\begin{itemize}
\item Compatibility of local Fargues--Scholze equivalences under
restricted-tensor-product gluing over primes.
\item Identification of the Whittaker model across all places
simultaneously.
\item Functional-equation-level compatibility between spectral and
automorphic sides.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Estimated paper length.} 80--120 pages.

\section{F2: Perfectoid $E_{2}$-Enhancement}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F2-details}

\begin{conjecture}[restatement of F2]
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F2-restatement}
The local Iwahori Hecke category $\mathrm{Hk}^{\mathrm{Iw}}_{GL_{2},p}$
carries a canonical $E_{2}$-monoidal structure from the
perfectoid Beilinson--Drinfeld Grassmannian over $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}_{p}$,
and the restricted tensor product
$\bigotimes'_{p}\mathrm{Hk}^{\mathrm{Iw}}_{GL_{2},p}$ inherits a
globally-defined $E_{2}$-monoidal structure.
\end{conjecture}

\paragraph{Local ingredients (theorem).}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Perfectoid affine Grassmannian (Fargues--Scholze, Scholze
diamonds).
\item Witt-vector affine Grassmannian (Zhu arXiv:1603.05593;
Bhatt--Scholze arXiv:1602.08693).
\item Equal-characteristic BD Grassmannian $E_{2}$-structure
(Mirkovi\'c--Vilonen + beilinson-drinfeld-grassmannian machinery).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Global gap (conjecture).}
\begin{itemize}
\item Diamond-gluing of Iwahori Hecke categories across primes.
\item Compatibility with Satake and Whittaker equivalences.
\item Perfectoid Dunn-additivity argument producing $E_{1}$-algebra
structure on the Hochschild trace.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Estimated paper length.} 60--80 pages.

\section{F3: Arithmetic Ben-Zvi--Nadler}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F3-details}

\begin{conjecture}[restatement of F3]
\label{v4-arith:app:C:F3-restatement}
$\mathrm{HH}_{\bullet}\bigl(\mathrm{Hk}^{\mathrm{Iw}}_{GL_{2},\mathrm{glob}}\bigr)\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{prim}}$
as $E_{1}$-algebras.
\end{conjecture}

\paragraph{Ingredients.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Conjecture F1 (for the spectral side identification).
\item Conjecture F2 (for $E_{2}$-enhancement of the local Hecke
factors).
\item Ben-Zvi--Nadler arXiv:0904.1247 (for the $\mathbb{C}$-version,
which F3 arithmeticises).
\item Explicit computation matching the Hochschild trace character
to $\zeta(s)$.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{What F3 accomplishes.}
\begin{itemize}
\item Unifies Gaiotto's CS presentation
(\Cref{v4-arith:thm:arith-CS-VA-correspondence}).
\item Unifies Bezrukavnikov--Bernstein's Hochschild presentation
(\Cref{v4-arith:thm:crowning-placement}).
\item Unifies Beilinson-solo's bar--cobar presentation
(\Cref{v4-arith:thm:bar-verifications}).
\item Proves the arithmetic six-window placement
(\Cref{v4-arith:thm:six-window-placement}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Estimated paper length.} 40--60 pages.

\section{Independent Conjecture: The $\mathsf{G}$-Row Complementarity}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:G-row-independent}

\begin{conjecture}[$\kappa+\kappa^{!}=0\Leftrightarrow\xi(s)=\xi(1-s)$]
\label{v4-arith:app:C:G-row-statement}
For the arithmetic Heisenberg $\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{prim}}_{\mathrm{Heis}}$,
the Vol~I derived-centre complementarity
$\kappa^{\mathrm{Ar}}+(\kappa^{!})^{\mathrm{Ar}}=0$ is equivalent
to the Riemann functional equation
$\xi(s)=\xi(1-s)$.
\end{conjecture}

This is \emph{independent} of F1, F2, F3: its proof proceeds
directly from the arithmetic Koszul-resolution computation of
Hochschild cochains on the arithmetic Heisenberg, using Tate's
adelic Poisson summation. A proof of this conjecture would inscribe
the arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row as a Vol~I Theorem~C corollary.

\paragraph{Estimated paper length.} 30--50 pages.

\section{Roadmap to Full Theorem-Status}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:roadmap}

Full theorem-status inscription of the arithmetic branch requires:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Paper (A):} Prove the $\mathsf{G}$-row independent
conjecture
(\Cref{v4-arith:app:C:G-row-statement}). This inscribes the Riemann
functional equation as a consequence of Vol~I Koszul duality.
\item \textbf{Paper (B):} Prove F3 conditional on F1 and F2. This
unifies the three main presentations.
\item \textbf{Paper (C):} Prove F1 (global arithmetic AG).
\item \textbf{Paper (D):} Prove F2 (perfectoid $E_{2}$-enhancement).
\item \textbf{Paper (E):} Combine (B)--(D) to unconditionalise F3.
\end{enumerate}

Papers (C)--(E) are long-term (multi-year). Paper (A) is
self-contained and could be the first inscription-quality paper to
come out of the arithmetic branch.

\section{Connection to Vol~III}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:vol3-connection}

Vol~III's $\mathsf{B}$-row $\kappa+\kappa^{!}=8$ (Mukai-enhanced K3
Heisenberg via Bruinier Heegner Chern-class reciprocity) has an
arithmetic avatar (F4, Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:open-frontiers}~\S3).
The combined programme --- arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row
(Paper~(A)) plus arithmetic $\mathsf{B}$-row (F4) --- would inscribe
two of the five Vol~I derived-centre-ceiling values as arithmetic
consequences of automorphic / Galois-deformation theorems. This
would move the programme from a chiral-algebra corpus to a
chiral-algebra-arithmetic corpus.

\section{Summary}
\label{v4-arith:app:C:summary}

Three hierarchically-ordered conjectures (F1 $\to$ F2 $\to$ F3)
plus one independent conjecture ($\mathsf{G}$-row). Each is
theorem-locally; each has a precise global gap; each corresponds
to a paper-length treatment with an explicit ingredient list. The
arithmetic branch as inscribed is a curated synthesis; these
conjectures are the inscription's natural continuation into
theorem-status.
