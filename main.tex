\begin{abstract}
Volume IV is the realization layer for the Modular Koszul Duality
programme. Its object is the functor
\[
    \mathrm{Real}(X,T)=(d,e),
\]
from a theorem \(T\) inscribed in a chapter \(X\) of Volumes I--III
to a source-disjoint verification decorator \(d\) and a compute engine
\(e\) whose expected values are not derived from the formula being
tested. The volume separates proof from realization: a
\texttt{\textbackslash ClaimStatusProvedHere} theorem supplies an internal proof, while
\(\mathrm{Real}(X,T)\) supplies an independent route capable of
detecting a tautological verification.

The current realization corpus includes the sharpened Vol~I shadow
tower \(c_S=-218/45\), the Borel radius
\(|\omega|^2(c)=c^2(5c+22)/[4(45c+218)]\), the
Riccati-in-\(U\) form of the Virasoro tower, the four-conductor split
\((K,K^\kappa,K_c,K_g)\), the \(p=691\) Chenevier determinant, and the
eight-row Gritsenko--Cl\'ery Borcherds spread. It includes the Vol~II
\(\mathsf{SC}^{\mathrm{ch,top}}\) heptagon and the finite-jet
Khan--Zeng PVA lane: the shifted-cotangent BV action and the
CME/PVA-Hamiltonian equivalence are constructed, while the all-loop
QME, reflected weight identity, boundary vertex algebra, and \(E_3\)
lift require the analytic SDR, image-choice Stokes, field-parity, and
self-image renormalisation package. It includes the Vol~III
two-stage Calabi--Yau-to-chiral factorisation
\(\Phi_d=\mathrm{Sp}^{\mathrm{ch}}_{\Sigma_{d-1},C}\circ
\Phi^{\mathrm{FA}}_d\), the K3 Hall--Drinfeld chiral-bialgebra crown,
Joyce--KPS compact-CY3 MNOP, \(S^3\)-framing on generic compact
Calabi--Yau threefold Lagrangians, and the
\(\mathfrak{so}(4,20)\) K3 Yangian trichotomy.

The arithmetic branch realizes the same discipline over
\(\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}\). Its terminal ledger isolates
one RH-equivalent obstruction, archimedean Tate positivity \(A\)-TP;
four upstream non-RH conjectures; one named sub-RH
Ramanujan--\(\mathrm{GL}_4\) hypothesis; and two density-zero
supersingular or icosahedral tails. The ordinary Meyer graph dual does
not carry point or finite-jet Hadamard residue data on the
critical-line support \(\Gamma_\xi^{\mathrm{crit}}\); any surviving
spectral-flow route must pass through a stronger rigged,
Paley--Wiener, de Branges, reproducing-kernel, nonlocal \(L^2\), or
BV--BFV boundary topology compatible with the post-Tate quotient.
\end{abstract}
