## Verdict

The full comparison does not close from the available sources.

The ordered incidence theorem is valid: the normalized simplicial
replacement computes the derived colimit over the ordered incidence
category. This is an algebraic `E_1` theorem.

The equality with full BD/CG arithmetic chiral homology is not proved.
The obstruction is not a sign issue. It is foundational: the existing
arithmetic object has no BD/CG Ran descent theorem whose exit category
is the ordered adjacent-contraction category. The naive Ran functor for
`Spec(Z)` is terminal; the enriched closed-place Ran object is discrete;
the finite-place diagonal has no BD-type infinitesimal collision
structure without extra prismatic/diamond data. The ordered category
also misses non-adjacent diagonals present in full BD factorization.

## Exact Target Theorem Attacked

The theorem the swarm asked to attack is:

**Target comparison theorem.** Let
`V = P union {infty}` with the auxiliary order
`2 < 3 < 5 < ... < infty`. Let `F_H` be the ordered coefficient functor
of Chapter 82, built from the per-place factors of
`\mathcal V^{prim}` and adjacent products. Then the canonical comparison
map

```tex
\Phi_{\mathrm{BDCG}}:
\operatorname*{L\,colim}_{\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)} F_{\mathcal H}
\longrightarrow
\int_{\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}}^{\mathrm{BD/CG}}
\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}}
```

is an equivalence.

This statement is stronger than Chapter 82 proves. Chapter 82 proves
the source of `\Phi_{\mathrm{BDCG}}` is computed by the ordered bar; it
does not construct the target as a full arithmetic BD/CG homology
object, nor prove the comparison map is an equivalence.

## Definitions

### Ordered Incidence Model

`V = P union {infty}` is given the auxiliary total order
`2 < 3 < 5 < ... < infty`. The category
`\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)` has objects finite ordered block decompositions
`B_1 | ... | B_r` of finite ordered subsets of `V`; morphisms are
generated by order-preserving contractions of adjacent blocks. The
merged block `vw` is formal. It is not a new arithmetic place.

Given per-place coefficients
`\mathcal H = {\mathcal H^{(v)}}_{v in V}` and adjacent products

```tex
m_{v,w} : \mathcal H^{(v)} \otimes \mathcal H^{(w)}
\to \mathcal H^{(vw)}, \qquad v<w,
```

define

```tex
F_{\mathcal H}(B_1|\cdots|B_r)
= \mathcal H^{(B_1)} \otimes \cdots \otimes \mathcal H^{(B_r)} .
```

The ordered incidence homology is

```tex
\int_{\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}}^{\mathrm{ord}}
\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}}
:=
\operatorname*{L\,colim}_{\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)}F_{\mathcal H}.
```

The ordered incidence bar is

```tex
B^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\bullet}(\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}})
:=
N_{\bullet}\operatorname{srep}_{\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)}(F_{\mathcal H}).
```

Here

```tex
\operatorname{srep}_{\mathsf C}(F)_n
=
\bigoplus_{C_0\to\cdots\to C_n}F(C_0)
```

and the normalized differential is the alternating sum of faces, hence
the alternating sum of adjacent contraction maps.

### Full BD/CG Model

For a smooth curve `X/k`, Beilinson-Drinfeld define the Ran prestack by
finite non-empty subsets, equivalently as a colimit over finite sets and
surjections. A factorization algebra is a `D`-module on `Ran(X)` with
factorization isomorphisms on disjointness loci and diagonal
cospecialization maps along collision strata. Chiral operations are
maps of `D`-modules on configuration spaces, not maps between isolated
stalks.

Costello-Gwilliam formulate the analytic/topological counterpart as a
prefactorization algebra satisfying cosheaf descent for Weiss covers.
For locally constant coefficients on a manifold, factorization homology
is the homology-type colimit over disks. For stratified spaces one
needs the corresponding constructibility and exit-category hypotheses.

An arithmetic BD/CG model on
`\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}` would therefore require:

1. an arithmetic Ran object with non-terminal closed-place points;
2. diagonal and disjointness strata with a descent topology;
3. a BD/CG factorization algebra on that object;
4. local constructibility with respect to a stratification;
5. an exit or disk category identified with the indexing category used
   to compute the homology.

These inputs are not in Chapter 82, Chapter 10, Chapter 6, Vol II, BD,
CG, or Francis-Gaitsgory.

## Proof of the Ordered Model Theorem

**Theorem.** Let `C = \mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)` and let
`F:C -> Ch_C` be the coefficient functor defined above. Assume the
adjacent products are associative so that `F` is a functor. Then

```tex
\operatorname*{L\,colim}_{C}F
\simeq
N_{\bullet}\operatorname{srep}_{C}(F).
```

Consequently

```tex
\int_{\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}}^{\mathrm{ord}}
\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}}
\simeq
H_{\bullet}\bigl(B^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\bullet}
(\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}})\bigr).
```

**Proof.** In chain complexes over `C`, every object is cofibrant for
the projective model structure used to compute homotopy colimits. The
simplicial replacement is the Bousfield-Kan bar construction for
`hocolim_C F`. Normalized chains on this simplicial chain complex
compute the homotopy colimit, hence the left-derived colimit. The faces
are induced by composition in `C`; since the generators of `C` are
adjacent contractions, the differential is exactly the alternating
adjacent-contraction differential of Chapter 82.

For the countable place set `V`, write `C` as the filtered union of the
finite subcategories `C_S = \mathsf{Inc}_{<}(S)` over finite ordered
subsets `S subset V`. Filtered colimits and direct sums are exact in
`Ch_C`; normalized chains and simplicial replacement commute with this
filtered union. Thus the finite-stage proof passes to `V`.

This proves the ordered model theorem. It proves no BD/CG comparison.

## Failed Comparison Attempt

The natural attempt is to forget the auxiliary order and send an
ordered block decomposition to the corresponding point of an arithmetic
Ran diagonal stratum. This would give a functor

```tex
q:\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)\to \mathsf{Diag}_{\mathrm{Ran}}(\overline C)
```

and the comparison would follow if `q` were homotopy final and if the
BD/CG factorization cosheaf restricted to `F_H`.

The attempt fails at three separate points.

1. **No full arithmetic BD/CG target is constructed.** Chapter 6 records
   that the naive Ran space of `Spec(Z)` is terminal and that the
   enriched closed-place Ran object is discrete. The factorization
   isomorphism over distinct primes is tautological, and finite-place
   diagonals have no non-trivial thickening without Fontaine/prismatic
   data. Thus the target of `\Phi_{\mathrm{BDCG}}` is not a constructed
   BD/CG homology object.

2. **The ordered category is not the full BD diagonal category.** In
   full BD factorization on a curve, the pairwise diagonal
   `z_1=z_3`, with `z_2` distinct, is a codimension-one collision
   stratum in `X^3`. In the ordered `E_1` model, only adjacent
   contractions are primitive. The collision of the first and third
   points is not available without moving past the middle point, hence
   without braid or symmetric descent data. Vol II explicitly treats
   this as the ordered `E_1` layer, not the Francis-Gaitsgory
   commutative/factorization layer.

3. **No local-constructibility/descent theorem identifies the two
   indexing categories.** CG factorization homology reduces to a colimit
   over disks only after the locally constant or constructible descent
   hypotheses. For a stratified arithmetic Ran object, one would need an
   exit-category theorem whose exit category is
   `\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)`. The available sources do not supply this.

The best available statement is conditional:

```tex
\text{BD/CG comparison}
=
\text{ordered model theorem}
+ \text{arithmetic Ran descent}
+ \text{ordered-to-unordered descent}.
```

Only the first summand is proved.

## Missing Hypotheses for a True Comparison

A proof of the target comparison requires the following hypotheses.

**H1. Arithmetic Ran geometry.** A non-terminal arithmetic Ran object
`\Ran_{\mathrm{Ar}}(\overline C)` whose strata are finite closed-place
configurations, with disjointness loci and diagonal/collision strata
carrying enough geometry for BD or CG descent.

**H2. Finite-place collision structure.** At each finite prime, a
replacement for the BD infinitesimal diagonal. Candidates are
prismatic, arithmetic `D^\dagger`, or Fargues-Scholze diamond
thickenings. The current minimal adelic structure supplies only `E_0`
stalks plus Adams operations.

**H3. Factorization descent.** A genuine factorization algebra or
cosheaf on `\Ran_{\mathrm{Ar}}(\overline C)` satisfying disjoint-union
factorization and Weiss/BD descent.

**H4. Local constructibility and exit-category identification.** The
factorization cosheaf must be constructible for a stratification whose
exit category is equivalent to the ordered adjacent-contraction
category, or else there must be a cofinal functor from
`\mathsf{Inc}_{<}(V)` to the actual exit/disk category.

**H5. Ordered-to-unordered descent.** The auxiliary total order must be
removed by braid or symmetric descent data. At the archimedean place
this can be mediated by the complex `R`-matrix story of Vol II. At
finite primes the manuscript has no corresponding p-adic braid descent.

**H6. Completion and restricted tensor exactness.** The countable
restricted product over primes, topological completions, continuous
duals, and filtered colimits must commute with the derived colimit and
with homology. Chapter 25 records related conditional Positselski
completion issues.

Without H1-H6, the comparison is a named residual obligation, not a
theorem.

\begin{proposition}[ordered-to-BD/CG comparison criterion]
\label{v4-arith:3d-acs:prop:BDCG-ordered-criterion}
\ClaimStatusConditional. Let
\(\Ran_{\mathrm{Ar}}(\overline C)\) be an arithmetic Ran object for
\(\overline C=\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}\), and let

\[
\int_{\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb Z}}^{\mathrm{BD/CG}}
\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}}
\simeq
H_{\bullet}\bigl(
B^{\mathrm{ord}}_{\bullet}(\mathcal V^{\mathrm{prim}})
\bigr).

\end{proof}
