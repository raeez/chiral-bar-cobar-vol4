## Exact Target Theorem

The target step has to be stated with its hypotheses separated:

**Centered-generator status theorem.**  Let `Theta_xi` be a closed densely
defined operator on a Hilbert space `H_xi`, and put
`S := Theta_xi - (1/2) id`.

1. If `S` is anti-self-adjoint, then `H := -i S` is self-adjoint.  Conversely,
   if `H` is self-adjoint and `Theta_xi = (1/2) id + iH` on the common domain,
   then `S` is anti-self-adjoint.
2. If, in addition, `Theta_xi` is a normal operator whose spectral divisor is
   the non-trivial zero divisor of `xi_R(s)`, then anti-self-adjointness of
   `Theta_xi - (1/2) id` implies RH.
3. Under the manuscript hypothesis `H-P^Ar`, this centered
   anti-self-adjointness is already built into the hypothesis, because
   `Theta_xi := (1/2) id + i H_HP` with `H_HP` self-adjoint.

Therefore the step closes only conditionally on `H-P^Ar`.  It does not close
as a new proof of RH unless one already supplies a source-disjoint construction
of the zeta-zero operator.

## First-Principles Route

The elementary functional-analytic equivalence is clean.  For a closed densely
defined operator `S`,

```text
S anti-self-adjoint
<=> S^* = -S
<=> (-iS)^* = -iS
<=> H := -iS is self-adjoint.
```

Thus, if one has already constructed a self-adjoint `H_HP`, the manuscript
definition

```text
Theta_xi = (1/2) id + i H_HP
```

immediately gives

```text
Theta_xi - (1/2) id = i H_HP,
```

and `(iH_HP)^* = -iH_HP`.  This is a domain statement only: the common domain
is `Dom(H_HP)`, and the closure/self-adjointness is carried by `H_HP`.

The non-formal route would require more:

1. construct a Hilbert space `H_xi` from arithmetic/geometric data;
2. construct a dense invariant core and closed operator `Theta_xi`;
3. prove `Theta_xi - (1/2) id` is anti-self-adjoint without using RH-equivalent
   positivity;
4. prove the spectral divisor or regularised determinant is exactly `xi_R(s)`.

Steps 3 and 4 together force RH.  Indeed, if `S` is anti-self-adjoint, then
`H=-iS` is self-adjoint and `Spec(S) subset iR`.  If the spectral divisor of
`Theta_xi = (1/2) id + S` is the zero divisor of `xi_R`, every non-trivial zero
has real part `1/2`.

## Circularity Attack

The proof does **not** close unconditionally.

The only unconditional self-adjoint operator currently constructed in the
Bost-Connes/KMS lane is the logarithmic modular Hamiltonian `N_BC`.  Its
spectrum is the additive semigroup generated by prime logarithms, not the
ordinate multiset of the non-trivial zeta zeros.  Tomita-Takesaki theory gives
a positive self-adjoint modular operator and hence a self-adjoint logarithmic
Hamiltonian, but it does not produce the Hilbert-Polya zero operator.

The formal zero-space construction is tautological.  If one defines a Hilbert
space with basis indexed by zeros and sets `H e_rho = gamma_rho e_rho`, then
self-adjointness is obtained only when the ordinates `gamma_rho` are real,
equivalently when `rho = 1/2 + i gamma_rho` lies on the critical line.  For
off-line zeros the same construction gives a non-self-adjoint multiplication
operator or silently replaces complex data by its real projection.  That is
RH input, not RH output.

The Li/Weil/de Branges routes do not remove the circle:

- Li positivity is equivalent to RH.
- Weil positivity is equivalent to RH.
- The de Branges/Hermite-Biehler upgrade for `E_xi` is equivalent to RH, and
  the stronger `HB+`/bounded-de Branges package used for VB* is strictly
  stronger than bare RH in the local manuscript analysis.
- The archimedean trace positivity `A-TP` in the HP-Li chapter is explicitly
  RH-equivalent.

The Connes/Deninger/spectral-flow route is the same obstruction in another
language.  Connes' trace formula and Deninger purity identify the desired
spectral object only after an absorption/purity statement whose content is the
critical-line placement of the zeros.  The manuscript's spectral-flow chapter
correctly records this as `A-TP^arch` / `H-P^Ar` strength, not as a theorem
deduced from Bost-Connes or finite-prime Frobenius data.

Verdict: any proof of anti-self-adjointness for the centered zero generator
that also identifies the determinant with `xi_R` is already a proof of RH.  If
it uses Li positivity, Weil positivity, de Branges `HB+`, archimedean trace
positivity, Connes absorption, or Deninger purity, it must be marked as using
an RH-equivalent or stronger hypothesis.

## TeX-Ready Repair Text

```tex
\begin{theorem}[Centered zero generator: exact status]
\label{v4-arith:rh:thm:centered-generator-status}
Let $\Theta_\xi$ be a closed densely defined operator on a Hilbert space
$\mathcal H_\xi$, and put
\[
  S_\xi := \Theta_\xi-\frac12 \operatorname{id}.
\]
Then $S_\xi$ is anti-self-adjoint if and only if
$H_\xi:=-iS_\xi$ is self-adjoint.  In particular, under
\(\mathrm{H\text{-}P}^{\mathrm{Ar}}\), where
\[
  \Theta_\xi=\frac12\operatorname{id}+iH_{\mathrm{HP}}
\]
with \(H_{\mathrm{HP}}\) self-adjoint, the centered operator
\(\Theta_\xi-\frac12\operatorname{id}\) is anti-self-adjoint.

Conversely, if a normal operator \(\Theta_\xi\) has spectral divisor equal
to the non-trivial zero divisor of \(\xi_{\mathbb R}(s)\), then
anti-self-adjointness of
\(\Theta_\xi-\frac12\operatorname{id}\) forces every non-trivial zero to
lie on \(\Re(s)=\frac12\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For any closed densely defined \(S\), the identity \(S^*=-S\) is equivalent
to \((-iS)^*=-iS\).  Applying this to
\(S_\xi=\Theta_\xi-\frac12\operatorname{id}\) proves the first assertion.
Under \(\mathrm{H\text{-}P}^{\mathrm{Ar}}\) one has
\(S_\xi=iH_{\mathrm{HP}}\), hence
\[
  S_\xi^*=(iH_{\mathrm{HP}})^*=-iH_{\mathrm{HP}}=-S_\xi .
\]
For the final assertion, the spectral theorem gives
\(\operatorname{Spec}(S_\xi)\subset i\mathbb R\).  Therefore
\[
  \operatorname{Spec}(\Theta_\xi)
   \subset \frac12+i\mathbb R .
\]
If this spectral divisor is the zero divisor of
\(\xi_{\mathbb R}\), this is exactly the Riemann hypothesis.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[No unconditional closure]
The theorem is a status theorem, not a construction of
\(H_{\mathrm{HP}}\).  The Bost-Connes modular Hamiltonian is
self-adjoint with logarithmic Euler spectrum, not zeta-zero spectrum.
Any source-disjoint construction of the above \(\Theta_\xi\) with
anti-self-adjoint centered part and determinant \(\xi_{\mathbb R}\) is the
Hilbert-Polya/Connes-Deninger residual itself.  If the argument invokes
Li positivity, Weil positivity, de Branges Hermite-Biehler positivity,
archimedean trace positivity, Connes absorption, or Deninger purity, it is
using an RH-equivalent or stronger input.
\end{remark}
```
