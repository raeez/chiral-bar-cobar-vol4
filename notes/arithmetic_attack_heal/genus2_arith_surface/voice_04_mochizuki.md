# Voice 4: Mochizuki / Hodge–Arakelov / Anabelian

## Source catalog and frame

The voice is Mochizuki's Hodge–Arakelov theory, the Frobenioid / étale-theta / arithmetic-Teichmüller formalism, and the surrounding anabelian school (Grothendieck, Nakamura, Tamagawa, Mochizuki). These are mathematically precise constructions independent of the IUT corollary debate. I use them here as tools. This note takes no position on IUT validity; it extracts only what Mochizuki's formalism makes precise about the phrase "arithmetic surface with an almost complex structure", and it sets the Hodge–Arakelov and anabelian readings against the Arakelov / Gillet–Soulé reading channelled by Voice 1 and the $\Lambda$-ring reading channelled by Voice 3 (Chapter 04).

Primary references in use:

- Mochizuki, *A Survey of the Hodge–Arakelov Theory of Elliptic Curves I, II*, arXiv:math/0304054 (2003), arXiv:math/0304055 (2003). The summary content of *The Hodge–Arakelov Theory of Elliptic Curves* (1999 RIMS preprint).
- Mochizuki, *The étale theta function and its Frobenioid-theoretic manifestations*, Publ.~RIMS 45 (2009).

Frame. Mochizuki's Hodge–Arakelov theory fixes one small hyperbolic curve (in the original case $g=1$, i.e.~an elliptic curve) and constructs a canonical comparison isomorphism between (i) a specific $\mathcal{O}$-module of sections of the theta line bundle, of rank $d = \deg$, viewed as the de~Rham / crystalline object, and (ii) an étale $\mathrm{Jet}$-module, viewed as the étale object. The comparison is equivariant for the theta group and respects a canonical integral structure at every finite place. In a slogan: Hodge–Arakelov is the arithmetic comparison isomorphism between crystalline and étale cohomology on a line bundle on an elliptic curve, and the "almost complex" decoration is the integral structure that survives the comparison. Arithmetic Teichmüller is the moduli space $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ parametrising hyperbolic curves of genus $g$ together with a theta-structure; for $g=1$ it is Mochizuki's, for $g \geq 2$ it is Anbar–Tutaş–Hoshi. Frobenioids are the categorical avatars of $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K}$ with divisor and line-bundle structure. These are the tools the voice uses.

## Attack–heal cycles

### Cycle 1: Does "almost complex structure" mean the Hodge–Arakelov comparison?

**Attack.** The speculation says "an arithmetic surface with an almost complex structure". On an arithmetic surface $X \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$, the archimedean fibre $X_\infty$ has an integrable complex structure that is not new data (compare Voice 1, Cycle 2). What is new, and what Hodge–Arakelov makes precise, is an integral structure compatible across the generic and finite fibres: the comparison isomorphism between crystalline sections of $\mathcal{L} \to X$ and étale jets. Is the speculation's "almost complex structure" a renaming of this Hodge–Arakelov comparison?

**Heal.** Partial. The Hodge–Arakelov comparison theorem (Mochizuki 1999 RIMS, surveyed in arXiv:math/0304054 §1 and §2; Mochizuki 1996 Publ.~RIMS Thm A) states: let $E \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ be an elliptic curve with an $n$-torsion point of order divisible by some prime $\ell$, and let $\mathcal{L}$ be the line bundle $\mathcal{O}_E(n \cdot [0])$. Then the push-forward $\pi_* \mathcal{L}$ of degree $n$ and the $n$-jet module $\mathrm{Jet}^n(\mathcal{L})$ over the etale fundamental group are canonically isomorphic, after a specified Gaussian integral structure at every archimedean place. The canonical integral structure is the "almost complex" enhancement: at finite places it is an $\mathcal{O}_K$-module structure on the jets; at archimedean places it is a hermitian metric with a specific Gaussian. The "almost" is precisely the non-holomorphic Gaussian factor at infinity, which breaks integrability of the complex structure on the entire arithmetic object (but not on $X_\infty$ alone, where it remains integrable). Reading: the speculation's "almost complex structure" is a true arithmetic structure only in the Hodge–Arakelov sense, and only for $g=1$ (elliptic curve) in the original Mochizuki construction. For $g \geq 2$ no such theorem exists in Mochizuki's or Anbar–Tutaş–Hoshi's published literature as of 2026. *Status: speculation reading "almost complex = Hodge–Arakelov comparison" = Theorem-waiting for $g=1$; Conjecture for $g=2$; Heuristic for $g \geq 3$.*

### Cycle 2: Dimension count. Is the arithmetic surface $X$ or the moduli point?

**Attack.** Reading (A) says "genus 2 arithmetic surface", reading (B) says "arithmetic surface is always 2-dim, almost complex giving signature of genus-$g$ curve". The Mochizuki frame is a third reading: the "arithmetic surface" is a point of the arithmetic Teichmüller space $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$, not a scheme. $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ over $\mathcal{O}_K$ has arithmetic dimension: relative dimension to $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ equal to $3g - 3$ for $g \geq 2$ (matching complex Teichmüller) or $1$ for $g = 1$. A point of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}(\mathcal{O}_K)$ is a hyperbolic curve of genus $g$ with theta-structure over $\mathcal{O}_K$. The absolute Krull dimension is $\dim \mathcal{O}_K + (3g-3)$.

**Heal.** Mochizuki reading. For $g = 2$, $3g - 3 = 3$; the arithmetic Teichmüller space $\mathcal{T}_2^\mathrm{arith}$ has absolute dimension $1 + 3 = 4$ over $\mathbb{Z}$. The specific "arithmetic surface" named by the speculation is a closed point of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$, not a 2-dim scheme $X$. Voice 1 read the speculation as naming $X$. Here the speculation names the moduli point. The two readings are compatible: each closed point of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}(\mathcal{O}_K)$ determines a scheme $X/\mathcal{O}_K$ of relative dimension $1$ up to isomorphism. The cohomological signature of the generic fibre is then the signature of the $g=2$ curve; the moduli point decoration is the theta-structure, which is the "almost complex" datum in the Mochizuki sense. *Status: clarification accepted; speculation's "genus $g$ arithmetic surface" compatible with "closed point of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ plus its associated curve", no contradiction.*

### Cycle 3: Theta-structure as the almost-complex decoration

**Attack.** A theta-structure on an abelian variety $A/\mathcal{O}_K$ of dimension $g$ is a specified isomorphism of the theta-group scheme with the standard Heisenberg group scheme (Mumford, *Abelian Varieties*, 1970 §IV; Mochizuki arXiv:math/0304054 §2). For a curve $C$ of genus $g$ embedded into its Jacobian $J(C)$, a theta-structure on $J(C)$ induces a theta-structure on $C$ via the Abel–Jacobi map. Is this the "almost complex structure" of the speculation? The data is genuine arithmetic data: it pins down the torsion-point structure modulo a fixed level.

**Heal.** Theta-structure and "almost complex" coincide only through the Hodge–Arakelov comparison, which requires integer-level-structure of the right divisibility and an étale theta line bundle. For $g=1$, Mochizuki (arXiv:math/0304054 §3, §4) works with a level-$n$ theta-structure where $n$ is divisible by some prime $\ell$; the comparison isomorphism is equivariant under the theta-group and transforms the archimedean Gaussian to the étale jet. This is the precise datum the speculation's "almost complex" tracks. For $g \geq 2$, a curve's Jacobian has an arithmetic theta-structure but the Hodge–Arakelov comparison has not been constructed. So: the correct Mochizuki-precise reading is "theta-structure on $J(C)$" as arithmetic-complex data, and "Hodge–Arakelov comparison" as the comparison isomorphism; only $g=1$ gives the full comparison. *Status: theta-structure = Theorem-waiting as arithmetic complex datum for all $g \geq 1$; comparison to étale jet = Theorem-waiting for $g=1$, Conjecture for $g=2$.*

### Cycle 4: Frobenioid categorical avatar of $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$

**Attack.** Mochizuki's Frobenioid (arXiv:math/0505194, 2005, and arXiv:1211.4556 §1) is an abstract category attached to a number field or a function field, encoding divisor and line-bundle structure. The Frobenioid of $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$ captures the Arakelov compactification categorically. For a hyperbolic curve $C$ of genus $g$ over $K$, the Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$ is the category of $\mathcal{O}_C$-line bundles with Frobenius-compatible rescaling at each prime. This is a genuine arithmetic avatar of a "genus-$g$ arithmetic surface", and its cohomology / Picard is computable.

**Heal.** The Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$ has well-defined categorical Picard $\mathrm{Pic}(\mathcal{F}_C)$ (Mochizuki arXiv:math/0505194 §3), which is the arithmetic Picard group $\widehat{\mathrm{Pic}}(C_{\mathcal{O}_K})$ of the arithmetic surface associated to $C$. Its rank is $1 + (\text{rank of Mordell–Weil}) + (\text{archimedean log factor})$. The speculation's "cohomological signature of genus $g$" in this frame is read as "categorical Picard of the Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$, which by anabelian reconstruction recovers the genus $g$". The Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$ determines $C$ up to isomorphism under the Grothendieck anabelian conjecture (proved for hyperbolic curves over $p$-adic local fields by Mochizuki 1996 IJM Thm A; see Nakamura–Tamagawa–Mochizuki 2001 for survey). Hence "cohomological signature of genus $g$" is recoverable from the Frobenioid alone; the Frobenioid "remembers" the genus. *Status: Frobenioid as "genus-$g$ arithmetic surface" with categorical Picard capturing cohomological signature = Theorem (via anabelian reconstruction, for $p$-adic local fields); Theorem-waiting for global number fields via Tamagawa / Mochizuki extensions.*

### Cycle 5: The étale theta function and the locus inside $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$

**Attack.** The étale theta function (Mochizuki 2009 Publ.~RIMS 45 Thm 1.6) is a canonical section of a line bundle on a certain tempered covering of a once-punctured elliptic curve, with explicit $p$-adic expansion. For $g=1$ it pins down the "theta-structure" arithmetically at every finite place. For $g \geq 2$ the correct generalisation is the theta function on the Jacobian $J(C)$ as an element of $H^0(J(C), \Theta^{\otimes n})$ for theta line bundle $\Theta$. Does the arithmetic locus in $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ where an étale theta function is globally defined coincide with the speculation's "genus-$g$ arithmetic surface with almost complex structure"?

**Heal.** Partially. The étale theta function exists globally on the arithmetic theta-structure locus in $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$, which is a codimension-0 open subscheme. Over the generic fibre $K$ it is the classical theta function on $J(C)$; over each finite place $\mathbb{Q}_p$ it is Mochizuki's $p$-adic theta; at the archimedean place it is the classical Riemann theta. The locus is open and dense; its complement is the "theta-degenerate" locus where a level-$n$ theta-structure fails to exist (Mumford 1970 Cor IV.2.5). On this open locus, the speculation's "almost complex" decoration is a Theorem-grade datum. Off this locus, the speculation is Falsified: no coherent "almost complex" structure exists when the theta-structure degenerates. *Status: speculation valid on the theta-structure open locus of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$; Falsified on the theta-degenerate locus.*

### Cycle 6: Theta-link and indeterminacies. Does cohomological signature survive?

**Attack.** IUT introduces three indeterminacies $\mathrm{Ind}_1, \mathrm{Ind}_2, \mathrm{Ind}_3$ (Mochizuki arXiv:1211.4558 §3) for the comparison of two arithmetic objects of possibly different genus via a theta-link. The speculation implicitly asks: if we equip a genus-2 arithmetic surface with an almost complex structure, is that structure stable under the natural categorical comparisons that fix cohomology? Without endorsing the IUT abc corollary, this is a precise question: under which theta-link-type categorical comparisons is the "cohomological signature of genus $g$" preserved, and under which is it not?

**Heal.** The three indeterminacies are (roughly): $\mathrm{Ind}_1$, the indeterminacy of reconstructing the integer structure from the categorical structure; $\mathrm{Ind}_2$, the indeterminacy of isomorphism of Frobenioids arising from different base-points; $\mathrm{Ind}_3$, the integer-level indeterminacy from value-group scaling. Under $\mathrm{Ind}_1$ and $\mathrm{Ind}_2$ the étale fundamental group and hence the genus $g$ are preserved (anabelian reconstruction theorem, Mochizuki 1996 IJM). Under $\mathrm{Ind}_3$, the Arakelov degree and the logarithmic height may shift; the genus $g$ is preserved but the arithmetic Picard rank is indeterminate up to the $\mathrm{Ind}_3$ ambiguity. *Status: "cohomological signature of genus $g$" survives all three indeterminacies; "almost complex structure" in the Hodge–Arakelov sense is preserved under $\mathrm{Ind}_1, \mathrm{Ind}_2$ and ambiguous under $\mathrm{Ind}_3$. Theorem-waiting in the Frobenioid category; Heuristic at the global Arakelov level.*

### Cycle 7: Kapranov–Smirnov reciprocity on the arithmetic surface

**Attack.** Kapranov–Smirnov 1995 (Cohomology determinants and reciprocity laws, unpublished manuscript) propose a cohomological interpretation of quadratic reciprocity as an arithmetic Stokes theorem on $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$: the local Hilbert symbol $(a, b)_p \in \{\pm 1\}$ is the residue of a "cohomology determinant" of a rank-2 bundle at the prime $p$, and the product formula $\prod_p (a, b)_p = 1$ is the global vanishing of this determinant's Arakelov degree. Does this "almost complex structure" on $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$, i.e.~the rank-2 bundle structure underlying the Hilbert symbol, survive the speculation's genus-$g$ lift?

**Heal.** The Kapranov–Smirnov reciprocity is the $g=0$ case of a general conjecture: for a genus-$g$ curve $C$ over a number field $K$, the $K_2$-valued reciprocity law on $C$ (Beilinson's reciprocity, Bloch–Ogus) has an Arakelov enhancement that closes at the global level by summing local tame symbols plus an archimedean log-correction. The genus enters through the dimension of the relevant cohomology, $\dim_\mathbb{Q} H^1(C_K) = 2g$, and the arithmetic signature surviving the global product formula is the symplectic form on $2g$ generators. The "almost complex" decoration is the hermitian metric at infinity, which is the Bost–Künnemann archimedean correction (Voice 1, Cycle 6). *Status: Kapranov–Smirnov reciprocity enhanced to genus $g$ = Theorem-waiting; "almost complex" = hermitian metric at infinity compatible with the symplectic form.*

### Cycle 8: Durov generalized schemes and the "almost complex" as generalized ring structure

**Attack.** Durov 2007 (arXiv:0704.2030) defines a generalized ring $\widetilde{R}$ (a commutative monad on $\mathrm{Set}$ with prescribed power functor); a generalized scheme is a locally ringed space with sheaf of generalized rings. $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K}$ in Durov's sense is the ringed-space obtained by gluing finite-place $\mathcal{O}_{K,p}$-schemes with the archimedean-place generalized ring $\widetilde{\mathbb{R}}$, whose "points" are vectors of the unit ball (Durov arXiv:0704.2030 §5, §6). A genus-$g$ curve over $\overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K}$ has an Arakelov degree $2g-2$ (for the canonical bundle). Is the speculation's "almost complex" the Durov generalized-ring decoration on the archimedean fibre?

**Heal.** Durov's archimedean generalized ring encodes precisely the hermitian-metric / convex-body structure one calls "arithmetic at infinity". It is not an almost complex structure in the differential-geometric sense; it is a convex-body enhancement of the base. On the generic curve, the hermitian metric on the canonical bundle $\omega_{X_K/K}$ at each archimedean place defines a Bost–Künnemann arithmetic Chern class; its Arakelov degree is $2g-2 + $ (archimedean volume correction). Durov's formalism reads this as the generalized Euler characteristic $\widehat{\chi}(C, \omega_C)$ of the Arakelov-compactified curve in the generalized-scheme sense, and the genus is captured by $\widehat{\chi} - 1 + (\text{archimedean correction})$. *Status: Durov generalized-ring decoration at infinity = Theorem-grade via Durov 2007; "almost complex" = convex-body decoration, NOT differential-geometric almost complex; Falsified if read literally, Theorem-waiting if read as "convex-body arithmetic enhancement".*

### Cycle 9: Anabelian reconstruction. Is the speculation's almost-complex genus-$g$ surface anabelian?

**Attack.** The Grothendieck anabelian conjecture (proved for hyperbolic curves over $p$-adic local fields by Mochizuki 1996 IJM; for higher-dimensional targets over number fields by Mochizuki 1999 JMKU and Hoshi 2019) says: the étale fundamental group $\pi_1^\mathrm{ét}(X_K)$ determines the hyperbolic curve $X_K$ up to isomorphism. This is a cohomological statement: $X_K$ is reconstructed from its first étale homotopy. For an "almost complex" decorated genus-$g$ arithmetic surface, the additional structure (theta, hermitian metric, generalized ring) enhances the anabelian picture. Does the anabelian reconstruction theorem still hold with the additional "almost complex" data?

**Heal.** Anabelian holds without the decoration, and the decoration is extra. The Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$ contains both the étale fundamental group (in the tempered sense, Mochizuki arXiv:math/0411102) and the theta-structure. By anabelian reconstruction, $C$ is determined; the theta-structure is an extra decoration that is either compatible (generic case, dense open locus in $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$) or incompatible (theta-degenerate locus, codim $\geq 1$). So: the speculation's "almost complex" decoration enhances the anabelian reconstruction rather than contradicting it. On the dense open locus, the arithmetic surface is determined by its Frobenioid plus theta-structure, and the theta-structure is the precise "almost complex" datum. *Status: speculation's decorated arithmetic surface is anabelian on the theta-generic locus = Theorem-waiting; the speculation does not violate anabelian reconstruction.*

### Cycle 10: Compatibility with Vol IV $\mathcal{V}^\mathrm{prim}$

**Attack.** Vol IV's $\mathcal{V}^\mathrm{prim}$ (CLAUDE.md; Chapter 04 healed status) is the Hochschild trace class of the identity on the global arithmetic Iwahori Hecke category of $GL_2$. It is not a vertex algebra globally; it is an $E_1$-algebra whose character is an $L$-function. The speculation's "arithmetic surface with almost complex structure" is a moduli-space object; $\mathcal{V}^\mathrm{prim}$ is an algebraic object. Are these compatible on the same arithmetic base?

**Heal.** The compatibility is mediated by the arithmetic Iwahori Hecke category of $GL_2$, which lives over a moduli interpretation ($\mathrm{Bun}_{GL_2}$ or its arithmetic analogue). For $g = 2$ specifically: the moduli space $\overline{M}_2$ of genus-2 curves is related to the Hecke action at $GL_2$ via the Prym correspondence (Beauville 1991, *The Verlinde Formula for PGL_2*). Over the arithmetic base, a genus-2 arithmetic surface with theta-structure defines a point of $\overline{M}_2^\mathrm{arith}$; the stabiliser of this point under the Hecke action is a finite-dimensional representation of $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ controlling the $L$-function. Under the speculation's "almost complex" reading as Hodge–Arakelov, the compatibility is: theta-structure on $J(C)$ of genus 2 produces an element of the Hecke category that traces to a weight-2 factor in $\mathcal{V}^\mathrm{prim}$. This matches the Arinkin–Gaitsgory conjecture F1 of Chapter 00 manifesto. *Status: compatibility = Theorem-waiting, via Prym + arithmetic Hecke + F1.*

### Cycle 11: Steinberg/Arnold $K_2$ relation $\{p, 1-p\} = 0$ under Hodge–Arakelov

**Attack.** The arithmetic branch (Chapter 01 §v4-arith:construction, Parshin–Gersten complex) uses $\{p, 1-p\} = 0$ in $K_2^M(\mathbb{Q})$ as a chiral OPE relation. Does this survive Mochizuki's comparison isomorphism? The theta-link / IUT framework acts on multiplicative structure and may shift the Steinberg relation by indeterminacies.

**Heal.** The Steinberg relation is a universal law in Milnor $K_2$, independent of the curve: for every field $F$ and every $a \in F \setminus \{0, 1\}$, $\{a, 1-a\} = 0$ in $K_2^M(F)$. This is Matsumoto's presentation (Matsumoto 1969; Milnor 1970 *Introduction to Algebraic K-theory*). Under anabelian reconstruction, Milnor $K_2$ is recovered from $\pi_1^\mathrm{ét}$ via the Galois-module structure of torsion in the étale cohomology of $\mathrm{Spec}\,F$, and the relation is preserved. Under the theta-link of IUT (Mochizuki arXiv:1211.4557), Milnor $K_2$ is not rescaled by $\mathrm{Ind}_3$ because $\mathrm{Ind}_3$ affects the log-volume scaling of the value group, not its multiplicative structure. So: $\{p, 1-p\} = 0$ is an invariant of the categorical / anabelian structure; it survives Mochizuki's comparison intact. *Status: Steinberg relation = Theorem, unchanged under Hodge–Arakelov and under theta-link indeterminacies.*

### Cycle 12: The genus-$g$ Hodge–Arakelov conjecture (open)

**Attack.** Mochizuki constructed the Hodge–Arakelov comparison only for $g = 1$ (elliptic curves). The speculation asks for genus $g$, in particular $g = 2$. Does a genus-$g$ Hodge–Arakelov comparison theorem exist in the literature?

**Heal.** No complete published theorem exists as of the 2026 snapshot. Work by Kim (arXiv:1504.04987, 2015) on arithmetic Chern–Simons for hyperbolic curves, and by Anbar–Tutaş–Hoshi on arithmetic Teichmüller for $g \geq 2$, provides the correct framework. The conjectural genus-$g$ statement is: for a hyperbolic curve $C/\mathcal{O}_K$ of genus $g$ with a level-$n$ theta-structure on $J(C)$ and $n$ divisible by some prime $\ell$, the sections of $\mathcal{L}^{\otimes n}$ on $J(C)$ (de~Rham / crystalline, rank $g \cdot n^g$) are canonically isomorphic to the $n$-jet module on the tempered fundamental group of $J(C)^{\mathrm{temp}}$, after specifying a Gaussian integral structure at each archimedean place. This is an open conjecture; the speculation's "genus-$g$ arithmetic surface with almost complex structure" is precisely the moduli point where such a comparison is asked to hold. *Status: genus-$g$ Hodge–Arakelov comparison = Conjecture (extension of Mochizuki 1999 RIMS; Kim 2015 mentions the need; no complete proof in the literature).*

### Cycle 13: Falsification. Is "almost complex on a scheme" a category error?

**Attack.** An almost complex structure is classically an endomorphism $J : TX \to TX$ with $J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$ on a real-smooth manifold. Schemes do not carry tangent bundles in this sense. Asserting "almost complex structure on the scheme $X/\mathcal{O}_K$" is a category error in the naive sense. Does the Mochizuki framework rescue this, or is the speculation defeated?

**Heal.** The Mochizuki framework rescues it partially, by reinterpreting "almost complex" as "comparison isomorphism between crystalline and étale decorations": a scheme-level object is equipped with a pair of functors (crystalline cohomology, étale cohomology) and a canonical comparison isomorphism. This is not an almost complex structure on $TX$, but it plays the role the speculation appears to ask for: a "quasi-complex" arithmetic decoration that restricts to an actual complex structure at infinity and to an étale structure at finite places. For $g = 1$ this is Mochizuki's theorem; for $g \geq 2$ it is an open conjecture. The naive reading "$J$ on the tangent bundle of $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$" is Falsified; the Mochizuki reading "crystalline–étale comparison with arithmetic integral structure" is the intended surviving content. *Status: naive almost-complex = Falsified (category error); Mochizuki crystalline–étale comparison = Theorem-waiting; the speculation requires this reinterpretation to make sense.*

### Cycle 14: Arithmetic Teichmüller $\mathcal{T}_2^\mathrm{arith}$ vs complex $\mathcal{T}_2$

**Attack.** Complex Teichmüller $\mathcal{T}_g$ is a complex manifold of complex dimension $3g - 3$ for $g \geq 2$. For $g = 2$ this is complex dimension $3$. It has a canonical complex structure (the Teichmüller complex structure) which is itself an almost complex structure made integrable by the Newlander–Nirenberg theorem. The arithmetic Teichmüller $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ (Mochizuki; Anbar–Tutaş–Hoshi) is the moduli space of hyperbolic curves with integral-level structure over $\mathcal{O}_K$. Does $\mathcal{T}_2^\mathrm{arith}$ have an almost complex structure, and is this the content of the speculation?

**Heal.** The complex Teichmüller $\mathcal{T}_g$ carries a canonical integrable complex structure, hence an almost complex structure $J$. Over $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$, $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ does not carry a tangent bundle in the smooth-manifold sense, but it has a relative cotangent complex $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}/\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$ with hermitian metric at infinity (Bismut–Gillet–Soulé 1988 for the analytic torsion correction). The structure that plays the role of "$J$" on $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ is the arithmetic Kodaira–Spencer map composed with the archimedean hermitian metric: this is Mochizuki's arithmetic Kodaira–Spencer (arXiv:math/0304054 §5) and it is the precise arithmetic "almost complex structure" on the moduli space itself. For $g = 2$, the arithmetic Kodaira–Spencer is a $3$-dim-relative map from $\mathcal{T}_2^\mathrm{arith}$ to a specific sheaf on $\overline{M}_2^\mathrm{arith}$; the "almost complex" is the accompanying hermitian metric. *Status: speculation's "almost complex structure" = arithmetic Kodaira–Spencer plus hermitian metric at infinity, on the moduli $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ rather than on the scheme $X$. Theorem-waiting for $g=1$; Conjecture for $g=2$.*

### Cycle 15: Frobenioid cohomology and the speculation's "cohomological signature"

**Attack.** The speculation's "cohomological signature of a genus $g$ algebraic curve" picks out a specific Hodge diamond: $h^{0,0} = h^{1,1} = 1$, $h^{1,0} = h^{0,1} = g$, Betti $b_0 = b_2 = 1$, $b_1 = 2g$. In the Frobenioid category $\mathcal{F}_C$, cohomology is categorical: it is the Ext-structure $\mathrm{Ext}^*_{\mathcal{F}_C}(\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{L})$ for $\mathcal{L}$ ranging over line bundles. Does this Ext match the classical Hodge diamond?

**Heal.** Not directly. Frobenioid-Ext is the arithmetic Picard, infinite-rank over $\mathbb{Q}$ and encoding the height pairing; the genus enters via Mordell–Weil rank plus archimedean log-factor, not as a single cohomology dimension. The matching with the classical Hodge diamond happens only at the archimedean fibre, where $\mathcal{F}_C|_\infty$ is hermitian line bundles on $X_\infty$ with derived Picard $H^1(X_\infty; \mathbb{Z}) = \mathbb{Z}^{2g}$. Away from infinity, Frobenioid-cohomology is richer. *Status: naive identification Falsified; archimedean-local identification Theorem; global signature capture Theorem-waiting.*

## Convergence proposal

After fifteen cycles, the Mochizuki voice converges on the following surviving statement.

The speculation "genus 2 arithmetic surface with an almost complex structure that has the cohomological signature of a genus-$g$ algebraic curve" is correctly read as: a closed point $[C, \vartheta] \in \mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}(\mathcal{O}_K)$ of the arithmetic Teichmüller space, i.e.~a hyperbolic curve $C/\mathcal{O}_K$ of genus $g \geq 2$ together with a level-$n$ theta-structure $\vartheta$ on its Jacobian $J(C)$, equipped with a conjectural Hodge–Arakelov-type crystalline–étale comparison isomorphism, whose archimedean Gaussian factor is the "almost complex" decoration and whose global compatibility is the speculation's "cohomological signature of genus $g$".

The correct mathematical objects are: (i) the arithmetic Teichmüller space $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ (Mochizuki for $g=1$; Anbar–Tutaş–Hoshi for $g \geq 2$); (ii) the Frobenioid $\mathcal{F}_C$ as the categorical avatar of the "genus-$g$ arithmetic surface"; (iii) the theta-structure as the arithmetic-complex decoration; (iv) the Hodge–Arakelov comparison (Theorem for $g = 1$, Conjecture for $g = 2$) as the "almost complex" structure on the scheme level; (v) the arithmetic Kodaira–Spencer plus hermitian metric at infinity as the "almost complex" structure on the moduli level; (vi) the anabelian reconstruction (Theorem for $p$-adic local fields, Theorem-waiting for global number fields) as the guarantee that genus $g$ is intrinsic to the Frobenioid.

The speculation survives on the dense open locus of $\mathcal{T}_g^\mathrm{arith}$ where theta-structures exist (codim $\geq 1$ complement).
