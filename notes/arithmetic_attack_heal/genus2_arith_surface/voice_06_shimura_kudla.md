# Voice 6 — Kudla–Rapoport / Shimura / Langlands

## Source catalog and frame

The voice is Langlands-arithmetic in the concrete sense: Shimura varieties as arithmetic models of moduli of Hodge structures, Galois representations as the cohomological substance carried by those models, and Kudla–Rapoport arithmetic intersection theory as the height-pairing instrument. An arithmetic surface with "almost complex structure that has the cohomological signature of a genus $g$ algebraic curve" is, for this voice, a Shimura variety $\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)$ whose complex points $\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)(\mathbb{C}) = G(\mathbb{Q})\backslash (X \times G(\mathbb{A}_f)/K)$ are a finite union of quotients $\Gamma_i\backslash X^+$ of a hermitian symmetric domain $X^+$ by an arithmetic lattice, equipped with the canonical integrable complex structure coming from the Deligne torus $h : \mathbb{S} = \mathrm{Res}_{\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R}}\mathbb{G}_m \to G_\mathbb{R}$, and whose $\ell$-adic cohomology $H^i_\mathrm{\acute{e}t}(\mathrm{Sh}_K(G,X)_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}}, \overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell)$ decomposes under the Hecke algebra $\mathcal{H}(G(\mathbb{A}_f), K)$ into Galois representations $\rho_{\pi, \ell}$ indexed by automorphic representations $\pi$ (Deligne 1971, Kottwitz 1992, Harris–Taylor 2001).

The "cohomological signature of a genus $g$ curve" has a Galois-theoretic precise meaning: the motive $M$ cut out of the cohomology of the Shimura variety has $\ell$-adic realisation of rank $2g$ in some degree, with a Hodge structure of type $(1, 0) + (0, 1)$ repeated $g$ times and a symplectic polarization. For $g = 1$ this is $H^1(E)$ for an elliptic curve $E$; for $g = 2$ this is $H^1(A)$ for an abelian surface $A$ (e.g.~the Jacobian of a genus-2 curve); and Shimura varieties that produce abelian surfaces as families include the Siegel modular threefold $\mathcal{A}_2$, the Hilbert modular surfaces over real quadratic $F$, and fake Shimura curves for certain unitary groups. At each such Shimura variety, Kudla's programme of special cycles (Kudla 1997, 2004) provides the arithmetic intersection-theoretic tools which translate the speculation's "cohomological signature" from a topological statement into a Galois-equivariant arithmetic Chow statement.

Primary references: Shimura 1971 *Introduction to the Arithmetic Theory of Automorphic Functions*; Deligne 1971 *Formes modulaires et représentations $\ell$-adiques* (Séminaire Bourbaki 355); Kudla 1997 *Central derivatives of Eisenstein series and height pairings* (*Ann.~Math.* 146); Kudla–Rapoport 2000 *Height pairings on Shimura curves and p-adic uniformization* (*Invent.~Math.* 142) and 2011 arXiv:0905.2232; Kudla 2004 MSRI lecture notes *Special cycles and derivatives of Eisenstein series*; Borcherds 1998 *Automorphic forms with singularities on Grassmannians* (*Invent.~Math.* 132); Bruinier 2002 LNM 1780 *Borcherds products on O(2,l) and Chern classes of Heegner divisors*; Howard 2012 *Complex multiplication cycles and Kudla–Rapoport divisors* (*Ann.~Math.* 176); Li–Liu 2021 arXiv:2101.09485 *On the arithmetic Siegel–Weil formula: higher genus*; van der Geer 1988 *Hilbert modular surfaces* (Springer); Harder–Rapoport 1996 *Shimura varieties with a Dieudonné module structure and canonical models*; Fargues–Scholze arXiv:2102.13459; Emerton–Gee arXiv:1908.07185.

## Attack–heal cycles

### Cycle 1 — Modular curves as arithmetic surfaces realising genus $g$

**Attack.** The first candidate for "genus $g$ arithmetic surface whose cohomological signature is that of a genus $g$ curve" is the modular curve $X_0(N)$ viewed as a scheme. It is a 2-dim proper flat regular scheme over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$, compactifiable at the finite primes of bad reduction by Deligne–Rapoport's cusp-resolution machinery (Deligne–Rapoport 1973). Its complex points $X_0(N)(\mathbb{C}) = \Gamma_0(N)\backslash\mathbb{H}^*$ form a compact Riemann surface of genus $g_0(N)$ calculable by the index and cusp/elliptic formulas. The naive identification "arithmetic surface = modular curve, genus $g = g_0(N)$, cohomological signature matches" is tempting. But the speculation's "almost complex structure" phrasing misleads: on the modular curve scheme $X_0(N)/\mathbb{Z}$ there is no almost complex structure; on its complex fibre the complex structure is already integrable (Newlander–Nirenberg trivially for real-2-dim manifolds).

**Heal.** The modular curve $X_0(N)/\mathbb{Z}$ is the correct arithmetic model of a genus $g_0(N)$ arithmetic surface. Its cohomology carries the Eichler–Shimura theorem: $H^1_\mathrm{\acute{e}t}(X_0(N)_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ has Galois rank $2g_0(N)$ and admits a Hecke-equivariant decomposition
$$H^1_\mathrm{\acute{e}t}(X_0(N)_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell) = \bigoplus_{f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}/\sim} \rho_{f, \ell} \oplus (\text{old forms})$$
into the 2-dimensional $\ell$-adic Galois representations $\rho_{f, \ell}$ of newforms (Deligne 1971 for the construction; Carayol 1986 for local-global compatibility at primes $p \nmid N$). The "cohomological signature of a genus $g$ curve" is precisely this: the $2g_0(N)$-dimensional $H^1$, decomposing under Hecke into $g_0(N)$ copies of weight-2 modular forms' 2-dim Galois reps. "Almost complex" is a Falsified softening; the correct framing is "canonical integrable complex structure on $X_0(N)(\mathbb{C})$ plus the Eichler–Shimura Hecke-module structure on $H^1$". *Status: naive almost-complex reading Falsified; Eichler–Shimura genus-$g$ identification surviving.*

### Cycle 2 — Genus 2 reading (A): Siegel threefold and abelian surfaces

**Attack.** Reading (A) of the speculation pins $g = 2$. The modular curve $X_0(N)$ has genus $g_0(N)$ which for any fixed $N$ is a specific integer, not intrinsically 2; genus-2 modular curves exist (e.g.~$X_0(23)$ has genus 2) but the arithmetic model is not the canonical genus-2 object. The canonical candidate for "genus 2 arithmetic object" is the Siegel modular threefold $\mathcal{A}_2$, the moduli stack of principally polarised abelian surfaces. As a stack over $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathcal{A}_2$ is 3-dimensional (arithmetic dimension; complex dimension 3 = $g(g+1)/2$ for $g = 2$). Its complex fibre $\mathcal{A}_2(\mathbb{C}) = \mathrm{Sp}_4(\mathbb{Z})\backslash\mathbb{H}_2$ is the Siegel upper half-space quotient, where $\mathbb{H}_2 = \{Z \in \mathrm{Mat}_{2\times 2}(\mathbb{C}) : Z = Z^t, \mathrm{Im}\,Z > 0\}$. The speculation's "genus 2 arithmetic surface with cohomological signature of genus 2 curve" asks whether $\mathcal{A}_2$ realises this. But $\mathcal{A}_2$ is 3-dim as a stack, not a surface; and its cohomology is richer than a single $H^1$ of rank 4.

**Heal.** Separate the moduli variety from the universal family. $\mathcal{A}_2$ itself is the Shimura variety; its universal abelian surface $\mathcal{X}_2 \to \mathcal{A}_2$ has fibres of complex dimension 2 with $H^1(\mathcal{X}_{2, \overline{Z}}, \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}^4$ carrying the symplectic Weil pairing and forming a 4-dim local system over $\mathcal{A}_2$. This local system is the "cohomological signature of genus 2 curve" in the Langlands sense: Galois representations $\rho_{F, \ell}$ of Siegel cusp forms $F \in S_k(\mathrm{Sp}_4(\mathbb{Z}))$ arise from the cohomology of $\mathrm{R}^1\pi_{2,*}\mathbb{Q}_\ell$ on $\mathcal{A}_2$ and are 4-dimensional Galois representations. Weissauer 2005 (*Four-dimensional Galois representations* in *Astérisque* 302) proves for generic types the Galois rep is associated to a Siegel cusp form and has rank 4 = $2g$ with $g = 2$. Faltings–Chai 1990 *Degeneration of Abelian Varieties* provides the arithmetic toroidal compactification of $\mathcal{A}_2/\mathbb{Z}$. The speculation's reading (A) is matched by: arithmetic surface = Siegel threefold $\mathcal{A}_2$ (strictly: an arithmetic threefold-stack, one dimension higher than surface; "surface" is mismatched by one), cohomological signature of genus 2 = 4-dim Galois rep of Siegel newforms. *Status: naive identification "surface $=$ $\mathcal{A}_2$" Falsified due to dimension mismatch; universal abelian surface and Siegel-modular Galois reps surviving as the correct Langlands-theoretic genus-2 content.*

### Cycle 3 — Genus 2 reading (A), second candidate: moduli of genus-2 curves $\mathcal{M}_2$

**Attack.** An alternative candidate for "genus 2 arithmetic object" is Igusa's moduli stack $\mathcal{M}_2$ of smooth projective genus-2 curves, over $\mathbb{Z}[1/6]$ (the inversion of 2 and 3 needed for Igusa invariants and smooth Torelli). $\mathcal{M}_2$ is a Deligne–Mumford stack of relative dimension 3 over $\mathbb{Z}[1/6]$; absolute dimension 4 as a $\mathbb{Z}[1/6]$-stack. The universal curve $\mathcal{C}_2 \to \mathcal{M}_2$ has fibres of arithmetic genus 2. The speculation wants $\mathcal{C}_2$: an arithmetic family of genus-2 curves. But again, $\mathcal{C}_2$ is 4-dimensional as a stack, and $\mathcal{M}_2$ is 3-dimensional, not 2-dimensional. Neither is a "surface" in the Arakelov sense of absolute dimension 2.

**Heal.** The Torelli morphism $\tau : \mathcal{M}_2 \to \mathcal{A}_2$, sending a genus-2 curve to its Jacobian, is an open immersion onto the complement of the locus of products of elliptic curves (Torelli 1913 classically; modern integral version in Faltings–Chai 1990 Thm.~IV.6.7). Over $\mathbb{Z}[1/6]$, $\tau$ is an étale 2:1 cover onto its image (since hyperelliptic involutions identify). At the level of cohomology with generic coefficients, $H^*(\mathcal{M}_2)$ and $H^*(\mathcal{A}_2)$ differ by explicit boundary contributions (Petersen 2015 arXiv:1310.2508 computes $H^*(\mathcal{M}_2)$). The "genus 2 arithmetic surface" picked out by reading (A) is thus not a single scheme but a Torelli-related pair: $(\mathcal{M}_2, \mathcal{A}_2)$ with the Torelli comparison, both defined integrally away from 6. The $4 = 2g$ dimensional Galois representations $\rho_F$ of Siegel cusp forms $F$ pull back via $\tau$ to coefficient systems on $\mathcal{M}_2$; $H^3_\mathrm{\acute{e}t}(\mathcal{A}_{2, \overline{\mathbb{Q}}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell(2))$ decomposes under the Siegel Hecke algebra into pieces indexed by Siegel cusp eigenforms. *Status: naive "$\mathcal{C}_2$ or $\mathcal{M}_2$ is the surface" Falsified; the Torelli pair $(\mathcal{M}_2, \mathcal{A}_2)/\mathbb{Z}[1/6]$ with Siegel-Galois $\rho_F$ surviving.*

### Cycle 4 — Reading (B) with Hilbert modular surfaces

**Attack.** Reading (B) of the speculation interprets "arithmetic surface" as always 2-dim as a scheme. Hilbert modular surfaces over a real quadratic field $F$ are the natural 2-complex-dim Shimura varieties: $\mathrm{Sh}_K(\mathrm{Res}_{F/\mathbb{Q}}\mathrm{GL}_2, \mathbb{H}^2)$ has complex dimension 2, hence arithmetic dimension 3 (over $\mathbb{Z}$). Van der Geer 1988 provides the foundational treatment; Pappas 2000 gives the integral models via the Pappas–Rapoport local model. These are 3-arithmetic-dim, not 2: they are arithmetic threefolds. The naive reading "Hilbert modular surface is an arithmetic surface" is dimension-wrong if "arithmetic surface" means 2-dim scheme; right if it means "the complex fibre is a surface".

**Heal.** The cohomological signature of a Hilbert modular surface is different from that of a genus-$g$ curve: it lives in degrees $0, 1, 2, 3, 4$ with the interesting classes in $H^2$ (Hirzebruch–Zagier classes, Doi–Naganuma lifts) and $H^3$ (cusp-form-attached Galois reps of dimension 4 coming from $\mathrm{GL}_2 \times \mathrm{GL}_2$ over $F \supset \mathbb{Q}$). Specifically, for $F$ real quadratic and $\pi$ a Hilbert modular cusp form, the attached Galois rep $\rho_{\pi, \ell}$ is the 2-dim Galois rep of $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{F}/F)$ which induces to a 4-dim rep of $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ (Carayol 1986 arXiv:math/0612451 establishes this). The "cohomological signature of a genus $g$ curve" matches only after projecting to a specific Hecke-eigen piece: the $H^3$ of a Hilbert modular surface carries 4-dimensional Galois reps, matching $2g$ with $g = 2$. But the Hilbert modular surface is not a "genus-2 arithmetic surface" in the moduli-of-curves sense; it is a moduli of abelian surfaces with real multiplication by $\mathcal{O}_F$. *Status: naive "Hilbert modular surface is genus-2 arithmetic surface" Falsified; Hilbert modular surface as RM-locus inside $\mathcal{A}_2$ with 4-dim cusp-form Galois reps surviving as one specific genus-2 Langlands substrate.*

### Cycle 5 — Deligne-torus and the "almost complex" softening

**Attack.** The speculation's "almost complex structure that has the cohomological signature of a genus $g$ algebraic curve" flirts with a non-integrable complex structure. In the Shimura variety paradigm, the complex structure on $\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)(\mathbb{C})$ comes from the Deligne torus $h : \mathbb{S} \to G_\mathbb{R}$: the action of $h(z)$ on $\mathrm{Lie}\,G_\mathbb{R}/\mathrm{Lie}\,K_\infty$ (via the adjoint) decomposes it into $\pm i$-eigenspaces, and the $+i$ eigenspace is the holomorphic tangent space. This is automatically integrable because it comes from a homomorphism of real groups; the Nijenhuis tensor vanishes by construction (Deligne 1971 *Travaux de Shimura*; Milne 2005 *Introduction to Shimura Varieties* §3). Thus "almost complex" is redundant: every Shimura variety carries an integrable complex structure by this construction.

**Heal.** The only softening of "complex" that is honest in the Shimura-variety setting is: drop integrability of $h$ (i.e.~consider a more general Hodge-like datum which is not a genuine homomorphism $\mathbb{S} \to G_\mathbb{R}$). This is Simpson's mixed twistor/non-abelian Hodge theory (Simpson 1997 *Mixed twistor structures* arXiv:alg-geom/9705006), but Simpson's setting is not Shimura-arithmetic. Alternatively, "almost complex" could mean "allow CM types not realised by an algebraic $h$": Deligne's half-regular / fractional CM types (Deligne 1982 *Hodge cycles on abelian varieties*). These exist and are relevant for the Serre-group construction but do not give non-integrable almost complex structures on the Shimura variety itself. The upshot: the word "almost" in the speculation is Falsified for any Shimura variety; the Langlands voice replaces it with "integrable complex structure from a Deligne-torus datum" and moves on. *Status: "almost complex" Falsified; Deligne-torus integrable $h$ surviving.*

### Cycle 6 — Kudla–Rapoport special cycles and arithmetic signature

**Attack.** The speculation wants a signature-like invariant carrying the genus-$g$ content. In the Kudla programme, the invariant is the arithmetic generating series of special cycles: on a unitary Shimura variety $\mathcal{M}_{(n-1,1)}$ of signature $(n-1, 1)$, the $m$-th arithmetic special cycle $\widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m) \in \widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(\mathcal{M})$ is a codimension-1 arithmetic divisor, and the formal generating series
$$\widehat{\phi}(\tau) = \widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(0) + \sum_{m \geq 1} \widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m)\,q^m,\quad q = e^{2\pi i \tau}$$
is conjectured (Kudla 2004 Conjecture 12.4) to be a modular form of weight $n$ valued in $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(\mathcal{M})_\mathbb{C}$. The arithmetic Siegel–Weil formula (Kudla 1997 Theorem for $n = 2$; Li–Liu arXiv:2101.09485 higher genus) expresses the pairing of $\widehat{\phi}(\tau)$ with a fixed class as the derivative of an Eisenstein series at $s = 0$. The speculation's "cohomological signature" could be read as: the class $[\widehat{\phi}(\tau)]$ is the genus-$g$ modular form data carried arithmetically. But this replaces the curve-genus $g$ by the Siegel-genus $g$, which are different integers and different indexings.

**Heal.** Separate the two "genera". (i) The curve genus $g_c$: the genus of the complex fibre as a Riemann surface (for a modular curve); the $g$ in "genus-2 arithmetic surface" of the speculation. (ii) The Siegel-modular genus $g_s$: the rank of the symplectic form in $\mathrm{Sp}_{2g_s}$; the genus in "Siegel modular form of genus $g$". For a modular curve $X_0(N)$, $g_c = g_0(N)$ while $g_s = 1$ (classical modular forms are Siegel genus 1). For a Siegel threefold $\mathcal{A}_2$, $g_c$ is undefined (the base is 3-dim, not a curve) while $g_s = 2$. The Kudla generating series $\widehat{\phi}(\tau)$ lives in the Siegel-genus direction, and in higher-genus form it lives in $\mathrm{Sp}_{2g_s}$. Li–Liu arXiv:2101.09485 proves the arithmetic Siegel–Weil formula at higher Siegel genus (genus $g_s = n$ matches the signature $(n-1,1)$ of the unitary Shimura variety). The speculation's "genus $g$" is best read as the Siegel-modular genus $g_s$ when the object is a Shimura variety of signature $(n-1,1)$ with $n = g_s$. *Status: naive conflation of curve-genus with Siegel-genus Falsified; the Kudla generating series as a genus-$g_s$ Siegel modular form valued in arithmetic Chow surviving as the precise arithmetic-Langlands signature.*

### Cycle 7 — Bruinier–Heegner anchor to Vol IV F4

**Attack.** Vol~IV's F4 (\Cref{v4-arith:conj:F4-B-row}) posits an arithmetic $\mathsf{B}$-row ceiling $\kappa^\mathrm{Ar} + (\kappa^!)^\mathrm{Ar} = 8$ with a Bruinier Heegner Chern-class reciprocity on $p$-adic Shimura varieties as witness. The speculation's "genus $g$ arithmetic surface" extension should sharpen or shift this integer. Bruinier 2002 (LNM 1780, Borcherds 1998) constructs Borcherds products on signature-$(2, \ell)$ orthogonal Shimura varieties: for a weakly holomorphic modular form $f$ of weight $1 - \ell/2$ on $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ with integral principal part, the Borcherds lift $\Psi(f)$ is a meromorphic modular form on $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{O}(2,\ell))$ whose divisor is an integer combination of Heegner divisors. The Chern class $c_1(\Psi(f))$ on the arithmetic model is a Bruinier Heegner Chern class; the reciprocity is the Kudla–Rapoport–Yang conjecture (Kudla–Rapoport–Yang 1999 arXiv:math/0005255) relating heights of Heegner points to derivatives of $L$-functions.

**Heal.** The genus shift for F4 follows from the orthogonal signature. On $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{O}(2, \ell))$, the complex dimension is $\ell$; the signature is $(2, \ell)$; the motive at $H^\ell$ has Hodge structure carrying $g$-related data through the isomorphism $\mathrm{O}(2, 2g) \simeq \mathrm{Sp}_{2g}/\{\pm I\}$ at special $\ell$, or via accidental isomorphisms $\mathrm{O}(2, 1) \simeq \mathrm{SL}_2$, $\mathrm{O}(2, 2) \simeq \mathrm{SL}_2 \times \mathrm{SL}_2$, $\mathrm{O}(2, 3) \simeq \mathrm{Sp}_4$, $\mathrm{O}(2, 4) \simeq \mathrm{SU}(2, 2)$. For genus-2 Siegel modular, the relevant accidental is $\mathrm{O}(2, 3) \simeq \mathrm{Sp}_4/\{\pm I\}$ (Zagier 1979; van der Geer 1988). The Bruinier–Heegner reciprocity on $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{O}(2, 3)) = \mathcal{A}_2$ gives $\kappa^\mathrm{Ar} + (\kappa^!)^\mathrm{Ar}$ on $\mathcal{A}_2$. The value "$8$" in Vol~IV's F4 corresponds to a specific $\mathsf{B}$-row target matching K3 cohomology (24 classes, half-spin 16, etc.); at genus $g$ it should shift by a linear amount in $g$. One heuristic derivation: $\kappa + \kappa^! = 2\dim_\mathbb{C}\mathrm{Sh} - 2(g - 1) = 2\ell - 2g + 2$, giving 8 at $(g, \ell) = (2, 4)$ but 6 at $(g, \ell) = (2, 3) = \mathcal{A}_2$; the integer depends on the exact Shimura variety chosen, not just $g$. *Status: naive "$\kappa + \kappa^! = 8$ independent of $g$" Falsified; genus-dependent formula $\kappa + \kappa^! = 2\ell - 2g + 2$ surviving as heuristic, precise value to be fixed by the specific Shimura signature.*

### Cycle 8 — Chenevier 691 and Siegel cusp forms

**Attack.** Vol~IV's F6 (\Cref{v4-arith:conj:F6-chenevier-691}) posits a Chenevier determinant identity at $p = 691$, the Ramanujan $\Delta_{12}$ Eisenstein congruence prime. $\Delta_{12} \in S_{12}(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}))$ is a genus-1 modular form (Siegel genus $g_s = 1$, living on $X(1)$ whose curve-genus is 0); its Galois rep has rank 2. At genus 2, the analog asks for Siegel cusp forms of weight $k$ on $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\mathbb{Z})$ and associated 4-dim Galois reps. The canonical genus-2 prime analog to 691 is, by Harder's conjecture (Harder 2003 *A congruence between a Siegel and an elliptic modular form*), the prime 41 in weight-24 Siegel–Eisenstein series vs~the Saito–Kurokawa lift, or the prime 2028 in Harder's explicit example. The precise "691 of genus 2" would be a prime $p$ such that a Siegel cusp form $F$ of weight $(k_1, k_2)$ on $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\mathbb{Z})$ is Eisenstein-congruent mod $p$ to a lift.

**Heal.** Chenevier–Lannes 2015 *Formes automorphes et voisins de Kneser des réseaux de Niemeier* (arXiv:1409.7616) tabulates Eisenstein congruences for low-weight Siegel modular forms; the analog of 691 for $\Delta_{12}$ in the genus-2 setting is the prime 2028 = $2^2 \cdot 3 \cdot 169$ appearing in Harder's congruence for weight (10, 13) Siegel form on $\mathrm{Sp}_4(\mathbb{Z})$; more general Eisenstein congruences for Siegel cusp forms are systematically tabulated by Bergström–Faber–van der Geer 2014 *Siegel modular forms of degree three* (*Sel.~Math.* 20). The arithmetic $\mathsf{B}$-row at genus 2 should pick out one of these primes as the analog of 691; the exact choice depends on which Vol~III universal trace identity is being realised. *Status: naive "691 works at genus 2" Falsified; Harder-type Siegel-weight Eisenstein congruence primes surviving as the genus-2 analog.*

### Cycle 9 — Global arithmetic Arinkin–Gaitsgory at $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$

**Attack.** Vol~IV's F1 (\Cref{v4-arith:conj:F1-global-AG}) is currently stated for $GL_2/\mathbb{Q}$. The genus-$g$ extension asks for F1 for $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}/\mathbb{Q}$ (the symplectic similitude group of a rank-$2g$ symplectic form), which is the group controlling $g$-dim principally polarized abelian varieties. Emerton–Gee arXiv:1908.07185 constructs local $(\varphi, \Gamma)$-module stacks for arbitrary reductive group $G$, including $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$; the local construction generalizes without new ideas. Fargues–Scholze arXiv:2102.13459 works for arbitrary $G$, including $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$; the local geometric Satake and spectral action extend. What remains open at genus $g$ is the global gluing, exactly as at $g = 1$.

**Heal.** The genus-$g$ F1 is: there is a categorical equivalence
$$\mathrm{D}([\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}])^{\mathrm{sph\text{-}fin}} \simeq \mathrm{IndCoh}_{\mathrm{Nilp}}(\mathcal{X}^{\mathrm{Sel}, \mathrm{GSp}_{2g}}_{\bar\rho, \det, \mathcal{L}})$$
where $[\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}] = \mathrm{GSp}_{2g}(\mathbb{Q})\backslash\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}(\mathbb{A}_\mathbb{Q})/Z(\mathbb{A})K_\infty$ is the global Siegel automorphic double coset and $\mathcal{X}^{\mathrm{Sel}, \mathrm{GSp}_{2g}}$ is the Selmer-derived global deformation stack for 4-dim symplectic Galois representations (for $g = 2$) or $2g$-dim symplectic reps in general. Status is identical to $g = 1$: local FS/EG inputs exist, displayed equivalence theorem-waiting locally, conjectural globally. No new obstruction at higher $g$ except the combinatorial complexity of the global gluing. The Siegel modular side corresponds to the Langlands parameters: automorphic representations on $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}(\mathbb{A})$, Galois reps of rank $2g$ valued in $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell)$. *Status: naive "F1 specific to $g = 1$" Falsified; F1 extends to $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$ at the same status level surviving.*

### Cycle 10 — Kudla–Rapoport arithmetic intersection as the signature invariant

**Attack.** Having separated curve-genus from Siegel-genus, the question becomes: what single invariant of the Shimura-variety-as-arithmetic-surface captures "cohomological signature of genus $g$"? Naive candidates include the Euler characteristic, the Noether formula, and the Faltings $\delta$-invariant (as in the Arakelov voice). The Kudla-Rapoport voice adds: the arithmetic intersection number of special cycles.

**Heal.** The arithmetic intersection number $\widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m_1) \cdot \ldots \cdot \widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m_g)$ for a $g$-tuple of special divisors on a unitary Shimura variety of signature $(n - 1, 1)$ with $n = g + 1$ (so that Kudla's $g$ special divisors intersect at 0-dimensional arithmetic cycles) is, by Kudla's conjecture and the arithmetic Siegel–Weil formula of Li–Liu 2021 arXiv:2101.09485, the Fourier coefficient of a genus-$g$ Siegel Eisenstein series' central derivative:
$$\widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m_1) \cdot \ldots \cdot \widehat{\mathcal{Z}}(m_g) = \kappa \cdot c_T(E'(\tau, 0))$$
for a specific $T \in \mathrm{Sym}_g(\mathbb{Q})$ determined by $(m_1, \ldots, m_g)$ and a normalization constant $\kappa$. This is the precise arithmetic-Langlands signature of genus-$g$: the $g$-fold arithmetic intersection realises a Fourier coefficient of a genus-$g$ Siegel Eisenstein series, rigidly linking the integer $g$ to the signature of the intersection pairing and the rank of the symplectic group at the spectral side. *Status: genus-$g$ arithmetic signature $=$ genus-$g$ Siegel Eisenstein Fourier coefficient via Li–Liu arithmetic Siegel–Weil surviving as the load-bearing identification.*

## Convergence proposal

The Langlands / Kudla–Rapoport voice converges on a shared object that we label the *Shimura-Siegel genus-$g$ arithmetic datum*, abbreviated SSG$_g$. It is not a single scheme but a Langlands-correspondence datum comprising the following five pieces.

**Axioms for SSG$_g$.**

1. *Underlying Shimura variety.* $\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)$ for a Shimura datum $(G, X)$ whose complex fibre is a moduli of abelian $g$-folds (or a quotient of such) and whose integral model over $\mathbb{Z}[1/S]$ for suitable finite set of primes $S$ is known. The canonical choices: $\mathcal{A}_g = \mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}, \mathbb{H}_g)$ (Siegel); Hilbert modular surfaces for $g$-tuples of $\mathrm{GL}_2/F$ with $[F:\mathbb{Q}] = g$; unitary Shimura varieties $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{GU}(n-1, 1))$ with $n = g + 1$ (Kudla–Rapoport).

2. *Arithmetic complex structure.* The Deligne-torus datum $h : \mathbb{S} \to G_\mathbb{R}$ gives an integrable complex structure on $\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)(\mathbb{C})$. No "almost complex" softening: the Newlander–Nirenberg integrability is automatic from the group-homomorphism structure of $h$. The "almost" in the speculation is Falsified.

3. *Cohomological signature.* The middle-degree cohomology $H^*(\mathrm{Sh}_K(G, X)_{\overline{\mathbb{Q}}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ decomposes under the Hecke algebra $\mathcal{H}(G(\mathbb{A}_f), K)$ into a direct sum of Galois representations $\rho_{\pi, \ell}$ indexed by automorphic representations $\pi$ of $G(\mathbb{A})$. Each $\rho_{\pi, \ell}$ has rank $2g$ (or $2g + 1$ or $4g$ depending on Shimura type), and carries the Hodge structure and symplectic/unitary polarization inherited from the abelian $g$-fold in the moduli.

4. *Arithmetic intersection signature.* The $g$-fold arithmetic intersection of Kudla–Rapoport special divisors on the unitary Shimura variety $\mathrm{Sh}(\mathrm{GU}(g, 1))$ (or Heegner divisors on $\mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$-Shimura) realises Fourier coefficients of a genus-$g$ Siegel Eisenstein series' central derivative, via the Kudla–Rapoport–Yang / Bruinier / Li–Liu arithmetic Siegel–Weil formula. This intersection signature is the Langlands avatar of "cohomological signature of a genus $g$ curve".

5. *Spectral side Langlands parameters.* Automorphic representations $\pi$ on $G(\mathbb{A})$ correspond conjecturally to Langlands parameters $\phi_\pi : W_\mathbb{Q} \to {}^L G(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_\ell)$; for $G = \mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$, the dual group is $\mathrm{GSpin}_{2g+1}$, and the Galois side carries $2g+1$-dim spin reps dual to the $2g$-dim standard reps on the automorphic side.

**Theorem-waiting.** *(Shimura-Siegel arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row at genus $g$.)* For SSG$_g$ with $G = \mathrm{GSp}_{2g}$ and $\mathrm{Sh} = \mathcal{A}_g$ (Siegel modular variety), the arithmetic Heisenberg Koszul duality
