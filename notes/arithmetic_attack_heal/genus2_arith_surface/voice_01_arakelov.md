# Voice 1: Arakelov (Faltings / Gillet–Soulé / Bost–Künnemann)

## Source catalog and frame

The voice is classical Arakelov geometry as it was built between 1974 and 2011. An arithmetic surface is a proper flat regular scheme $\pi : X \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ of relative dimension one, compactified at infinity by the archimedean fibres $X_\infty = \bigsqcup_{v \mid \infty} X(\mathbb{C})_v$ carrying a Kähler form invariant under complex conjugation. The complex structure on $X_\infty$ is integrable and canonical, not almost-complex in any non-integrable sense. The cohomological signature is not the Hodge diamond of $X(\mathbb{C})$ in isolation; it is the arithmetic Chow theory $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^*(X)$ of Gillet–Soulé (Publ.~IHES 72, 1990) with its real-valued height pairing, the arithmetic degree $\widehat{\deg}$ on $\widehat{\mathrm{Pic}}(X)$, the arithmetic self-intersection $\widehat{\omega}^2$ of Faltings 1984, and the arithmetic Riemann–Roch theorem of Gillet–Soulé 1992.

Primary references: Arakelov, *Izv.~Akad.~Nauk* 38 (1974); Faltings, *Ann.~Math.* 119 (1984); Gillet–Soulé, *Publ.~IHES* 72 (1990); Gillet–Soulé, *Publ.~IHES* 77 (1992); Soulé, *Lectures on Arakelov Geometry* (1992); Bost, *Potential theory and Lefschetz theorems for arithmetic surfaces* (1999); Bost–Künnemann, *Adv.~Math.* 223 (2010); Bismut–Gillet–Soulé *Commun.~Math.~Phys.* 115 (1988) for the analytic torsion side.

## Attack–heal cycles

### Cycle 1: Dimension count

**Attack.** The speculation says "genus 2 arithmetic surface: an arithmetic surface with an almost complex structure". Over Arakelov's definition, an arithmetic surface has absolute Krull dimension $\dim\mathcal{O}_K + 1$. For $\mathcal{O}_K = \mathbb{Z}$ this is $2$; for a number ring it is $2$ always, because every $\mathcal{O}_K$ is one-dimensional Dedekind. The word "surface" is genuine: as a scheme, $X$ is 2-dimensional. But the archimedean fibre $X_\infty$ has complex dimension $1$. The speculation conflates the scheme-level dimension with the complex-dimension of the archimedean fibre.

**Heal.** Fix the two layers. The ambient scheme $X$ is two-dimensional; its generic fibre $X_K$ is a one-dimensional smooth projective curve of genus $g$; its archimedean fibre $X_\infty$ is a complex curve (complex dimension $1$, real dimension $2$) of the same genus. "Arithmetic surface" is scheme-dimension; "genus $g$" is a property of the generic fibre. Arakelov 1974 is explicit on this point. The integer $g$ is a birational invariant of $X_K$, stable under the Arakelov compactification. Reading (B) of the speculation (arithmetic surface always 2-dim, almost complex structure giving cohomological signature of genus $g$ curve) is the closer approximation to Arakelov; reading (A) (genus 2 specifically) fixes $g = 2$ for the generic fibre. Neither reading asks for a 4-manifold. *Status: naive dimension confusion Falsified; layered dimension count surviving.*

### Cycle 2: Almost complex vs integrable complex

**Attack.** The speculation wants "an almost complex structure" on an arithmetic surface. On $X_\infty = X(\mathbb{C})_v$, any Riemann surface is Kähler and the almost complex structure $J$ satisfies $J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$ and is automatically integrable (real dimension $2$ forces Nijenhuis tensor to vanish). An almost complex structure on a surface adds no data beyond the complex structure. On the scheme $X$ itself, there is no almost complex structure over $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$: an almost complex structure requires a real tangent bundle, and the Zariski tangent bundle of $X$ over $\mathbb{Z}$ is not a real vector bundle in any natural sense. The category of schemes does not carry almost complex geometry.

**Heal.** There are three honest readings of "almost complex" for arithmetic surfaces, none of which are Arakelov-native and the first of which is trivial. (i) On $X_\infty$ itself, $J$ is already there and integrable; no new content. (ii) On a real-analytic realization $X^{\mathrm{an}}_{\mathbb{R}}$ spanning the arithmetic direction, one could impose an almost complex structure. This is Deninger's programme (*Ann.~Math.* 160, 2002): a hypothetical dynamical system or foliated space of real dimension $3$ on which Frobenius acts. It is not Arakelov. (iii) On the relative cotangent complex $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{X/\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$ equipped with a hermitian metric at infinity, one can speak of a ``hermitian structure at infinity'' which Bost 1999 formalises as an $L^2$-metric. This is the genuine Arakelov input and replaces ``almost complex'' by ``hermitian at infinity''. *Status: naive almost-complex-on-$X$ Falsified; hermitian metric on $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{X/\mathbb{Z}}$ surviving as the correct arithmetic-direction enhancement.*

### Cycle 3: Cohomological signature of genus $g$ at the archimedean fibre

**Attack.** ``Cohomological signature of a genus $g$ algebraic curve'' is ambiguous. For a compact Riemann surface $\Sigma_g$, the Hodge diamond is $h^{0,0} = h^{1,1} = 1$, $h^{1,0} = h^{0,1} = g$, totalling Betti numbers $b_0 = b_2 = 1$, $b_1 = 2g$, $\chi(\Sigma_g) = 2 - 2g$. The speculation asks the arithmetic surface to carry this signature. But which object on $X$ should have this signature? The Betti cohomology of $X(\mathbb{C})$ (which sits inside $X_\infty$ place by place)? The arithmetic Chow groups of $X$ as Gillet–Soulé define them? The de~Rham cohomology of the generic fibre? These are different objects and the naive identification fails.

**Heal.** Separate the three layers. (a) The archimedean fibre's Betti cohomology: $H^0(X_\infty) = \mathbb{C}$, $H^1(X_\infty) = \mathbb{C}^{2g}$, $H^2(X_\infty) = \mathbb{C}$ per archimedean place. Summed over complex places of $K$, this is the ``signature-at-infinity''. (b) The generic-fibre algebraic de~Rham cohomology $H^*_{\mathrm{dR}}(X_K)$: same dimensions as $H^*_{\mathrm{Betti}}(X_\infty)$ by the comparison theorem. (c) The arithmetic Chow groups $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^p(X)$: these are infinite-rank; they carry the height pairing; they are not $\mathbb{Q}$-vector spaces of dimension determined by $g$ alone. The speculation's ``cohomological signature'' matches (a)/(b) but not (c). The correct statement is: at the archimedean fibre, the cohomological signature is that of a genus $g$ compact Kähler manifold of complex dimension 1. The arithmetic Chow groups are a separate and richer invariant. *Status: naive signature-everywhere Falsified; signature-at-infinity plus richer Chow surviving.*

### Cycle 4: Arithmetic Hodge index theorem and the sign of the intersection form

**Attack.** On a smooth projective surface $S/\mathbb{C}$, the Hodge index theorem gives the signature of the intersection form on $H^{1,1}(S) \cap H^2(S,\mathbb{R})$: it is $(1, h^{1,1} - 1)$. For an arithmetic surface $X/\mathcal{O}_K$, Faltings 1984 and Hriljac 1990 prove an arithmetic Hodge index theorem for $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(X)_{\mathbb{R}}$ restricted to divisors of arithmetic degree zero: the intersection pairing is negative definite on the orthogonal complement of the archimedean fibres. The speculation wants ``signature of a genus $g$ curve''. But genus $g$ curves are $1$-dimensional; their ``intersection form'' is the cup product $H^1 \otimes H^1 \to H^2$, which is a symplectic form of rank $2g$, not a signature form.

**Heal.** The arithmetic Hodge index sits one dimension up from the speculation's target. For a family $X \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ with generic fibre of genus $g$, the signature structure lives on $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(X)_{\mathbb{R}}$ and reads: intersection pairing is $(1, \infty)$-dimensional negative-semi-definite on degree-zero classes modulo the kernel coming from vertical fibres. The ``genus $g$'' enters through the Jacobian: $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(X)^0_{\mathbb{R}} / (\text{kernel})$ contains the Arakelov Jacobian, a real torus of dimension $g$ per archimedean place. The symplectic form on $H^1(X_\infty)$ lifts to the Arakelov Jacobian as the Néron–Tate height pairing. Faltings–Hriljac: for $D, D'$ divisors of degree zero on $X$, $\widehat{D} \cdot \widehat{D}' = -\langle D, D' \rangle_{\mathrm{NT}}$. *Status: ``signature of genus $g$ curve'' imprecise; corrected to ``arithmetic Hodge index on $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1$ with Néron–Tate negative-semidefinite pairing carrying the $2g$-dimensional symplectic structure of the generic fibre''.*

### Cycle 5: Arithmetic Riemann–Roch and the genus invariant

**Attack.** Gillet–Soulé 1992 prove the arithmetic Riemann–Roch theorem: for $\overline{E}$ a hermitian vector bundle on $X$,
\[
\widehat{\deg}\, R\pi_* \overline{E} = \widehat{\mathrm{ch}}(\overline{E}) \cdot \widehat{\mathrm{Td}}(\overline{\mathrm{T}}_{X/S}) - (0, T(X_\infty, E_\infty)),
\]
where $T(X_\infty, E_\infty)$ is the Bismut–Gillet–Soulé analytic torsion (Bismut–Gillet–Soulé, *Commun.~Math.~Phys.* 115, 1988). The Euler characteristic $\chi(X_K, \mathcal{O}) = 1 - g$ for a genus $g$ curve. The speculation asks: is ``genus $g$'' captured by a single invariant of the arithmetic surface? Naively one would say ``arithmetic $1 - g$ = arithmetic Euler characteristic''; this is wrong because $\widehat{\deg} R\pi_*\mathcal{O}_X$ has an archimedean correction involving the analytic torsion, and the answer is not simply $1 - g$.

**Heal.** The correct arithmetic avatar of ``Euler characteristic = $1 - g$'' is Faltings' $\delta$-invariant of the Riemann surface $X_\infty$ (Faltings 1984 \S2): for a compact Riemann surface of genus $g \geq 1$, $\delta(X_\infty)$ is a real-valued invariant computable from the regularised determinant of the Laplacian $\det'\Delta$ on $(X_\infty, \mu_\mathrm{Ar})$ and the Arakelov volume form $\mu_\mathrm{Ar}$. Faltings proves
\[
\widehat{\deg}\det R\pi_*\omega_{X/S} = \tfrac{1}{12}(\widehat{\omega}^2 + \delta_\mathrm{Ar})
\]
(arithmetic Noether formula; Faltings 1984, Thm 5). Here $\widehat{\omega}^2$ is the arithmetic self-intersection of the relative dualising sheaf and $\delta_\mathrm{Ar} = \sum_{v|\infty} \delta(X_{\infty,v})$. The genus $g$ shows up because for $g \geq 2$ the relative dualising sheaf is the correct pivot and $\deg\omega_{X_K/K} = 2g - 2$. Moret-Bailly 1990 (*Compos.~Math.* 76) extends the computation. *Status: naive ``arithmetic Euler characteristic $= 1 - g$'' Falsified; arithmetic Noether formula surviving with $\delta$-invariant correction.*

### Cycle 6: Bost–Künnemann product formula and the ``Künneth of arithmetic surfaces''

**Attack.** For smooth projective surfaces $S_1, S_2 /\mathbb{C}$, the Künneth formula gives $H^*(S_1 \times S_2) = H^*(S_1) \otimes H^*(S_2)$. The speculation's reading (B) suggests: the arithmetic surface carries the cohomological structure of a genus $g$ curve, i.e.~one half of a Künneth ``genus $g \times \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$''. Bost–Künnemann 2010 (*Adv.~Math.* 223) prove an arithmetic product formula relating heights of hermitian vector bundles on products. Does it factor as ``arithmetic direction'' $\otimes$ ``genus-$g$ direction''?

**Heal.** No such factorisation holds in general; it holds for the archimedean-local Hodge–Betti theory but not for the global arithmetic Chow theory. Bost–Künnemann's formula is
\[
\widehat{c}_1(\overline{E}_1 \boxtimes \overline{E}_2) = \widehat{c}_1(\overline{E}_1) \boxtimes 1 + 1 \boxtimes \widehat{c}_1(\overline{E}_2) + (\text{archimedean correction terms})
\]
in $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(X_1 \times_{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K} X_2)$. The archimedean correction comes from Bott–Chern forms and does not vanish. An arithmetic surface does not factor as ``genus $g$ curve $\times_\mathrm{Spec\,\mathbb{Z}} \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$'' at the level of arithmetic Chow; only at the level of ``archimedean-Hodge tensor product modulo Bott–Chern corrections'' does this hold. *Status: naive Künneth factorisation Falsified; Bost–Künnemann with explicit Bott–Chern correction surviving as the substitute.*

### Cycle 7: The archimedean place: Green functions, $*$-product, regularized Laplacian

**Attack.** Arakelov's archimedean machinery demands more than a Kähler metric on $X_\infty$. Every divisor $D$ on $X_\infty$ has a Green's function $g_D$ satisfying $\frac{i}{\pi}\partial\bar\partial g_D + \delta_D = \mu_\mathrm{Ar}$, where $\mu_\mathrm{Ar}$ is the Arakelov (1,1)-form. The $*$-product $g_D * g_{D'}$ defines the archimedean contribution to the intersection number. The speculation's ``almost complex structure giving cohomological signature of genus $g$'' is silent on this machinery. Without Green's functions and the $*$-product, there is no arithmetic intersection theory.

**Heal.** The archimedean structure is not an ``almost complex structure'' but a Kähler metric $\mu_\mathrm{Ar}$ together with the associated Green's function theory and regularised analytic torsion. Faltings 1984 fixes $\mu_\mathrm{Ar}$ as the Arakelov canonical form: $\mu_\mathrm{Ar} = \frac{i}{2g}\sum_j \omega_j \wedge \bar\omega_j$ where $\{\omega_j\}$ is an orthonormal basis of holomorphic $1$-forms on $X_\infty$ relative to the Hodge inner product $\langle \omega, \eta\rangle = \frac{i}{2}\int_{X_\infty}\omega \wedge \bar\eta$. The ``genus $g$'' enters through the dimension of the space of holomorphic $1$-forms. The regularised determinant $\det'\Delta_{\mu_\mathrm{Ar}}$ is the zeta-regularised product of non-zero Laplacian eigenvalues and furnishes $\delta(X_\infty)$ up to explicit constants (Faltings 1984 \S2; Soulé 1992, \S5). *Status: ``almost complex structure'' replaced by ``Arakelov Kähler metric plus Green function theory plus analytic torsion''.*

### Cycle 8: Cross-volume anchor: $\kappa + \kappa^! = 0 \Leftrightarrow \xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ for a family of genus-$g$ curves

**Attack.** Vol IV's arithmetic branch load-bearing theorem is the arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row complementarity equivalent to the Riemann functional equation $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$ (\Cref{v4-arith:thm:P1-resolution}). The speculation's ``genus $g$ arithmetic surface'' naturally suggests: is there an analogous $\xi_g$ or $L_g$ whose functional equation is encoded by the genus-$g$ arithmetic version? The naive guess: each genus-$g$ curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ has a Hasse–Weil $L$-function $L(s, X_K)$ with conjectural functional equation involving the conductor $N_{X_K}$, with $\Lambda(s, X_K) = N_{X_K}^{s/2} \Gamma_\mathbb{C}(s)^g L(s, X_K)$ satisfying $\Lambda(s) = \epsilon \Lambda(2 - s)$. This is the automorphic avatar. But the speculation wants an arithmetic-surface Koszul self-duality story, not an $L$-function functional equation for a fixed $X_K$.

**Heal.** The right framing: for a family $\pi : X \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ with generic fibre of genus $g$, the arithmetic Heisenberg construction of Vol~IV attaches to the archimedean fibre an archimedean chiral algebra $\mathcal{V}^{(\infty)}_g$ of central charge depending on $g$. The relative cotangent complex $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{X/\mathbb{Z}}$ is a rank-$1$ hermitian module, so the per-prime Fock $\mathcal{H}^{(p)}$ of the Heisenberg gets replaced by a rank-$g$ Fock $\mathcal{H}^{(p)}_g$: the Satake parameters now live in $GL_{2g}$ (the Tate module is $2g$-dimensional), not $GL_2$. The functional equation becomes
\[
\xi_g(s) = \xi_g(2 - s)
\]
(with the shift $2 - s$ because the generic fibre has relative dimension $1$ and the motive has weight $1$; this is the symplectic functional equation of Weil's conjecture for abelian varieties, specialised to Jacobians). The Vol~IV Koszul self-duality $\kappa^{\mathrm{Ar}}_g + (\kappa^!)^{\mathrm{Ar}}_g = 0$ at genus $g$ would imply $\xi_g(s) = \xi_g(2 - s)$ as a generalisation of P1. *Status: genus-$g$ generalisation of the $\mathsf{G}$-row theorem: Theorem-waiting, strictly wider than the $g = 0$ case of Vol~IV's load-bearing statement.*

## Convergence proposal

The shared object onto which the six voices should converge is the \emph{Arakelov–Arithmetic Genus-$g$ Fibration}, abbreviated AAGF$_g$. It is not a new object; it is the honest Arakelov-geometric target of the speculation, stated in four axioms plus one theorem-waiting.

\textbf{Axioms for AAGF$_g$}.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Underlying arithmetic 2-fold.} $X$ is a proper flat regular scheme of absolute dimension $2$ over $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$, with structure morphism $\pi : X \to \mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K$ of relative dimension $1$, whose generic fibre $X_K$ is a smooth projective curve of genus $g \geq 0$.
\item \emph{Complex/hermitian structure at infinity.} The base change $X_\infty = \bigsqcup_{v|\infty} X(\mathbb{C})_v$ carries its canonical integrable complex structure $J_\infty$ (not almost complex: the non-integrable case is Falsified by Newlander–Nirenberg on real-$2$-manifolds) together with a Kähler form, which we fix as the Arakelov form $\mu_\mathrm{Ar,g}$ of Faltings 1984. The relative cotangent complex $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{X/\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathcal{O}_K}$ carries an $L^2$-hermitian metric at infinity induced by $\mu_\mathrm{Ar,g}$.
\item \emph{Cohomological signature.} The arithmetic cohomological signature is the pair (Betti-at-infinity, arithmetic Chow):
\begin{enumerate}
\item per-archimedean-place Hodge diamond $h^{0,0} = h^{1,1} = 1$, $h^{1,0} = h^{0,1} = g$ on $X(\mathbb{C})_v$;
\item $\widehat{\mathrm{CH}}^1(X)$ equipped with arithmetic intersection pairing and Faltings–Hriljac arithmetic Hodge index, whose restriction to degree-zero classes mod vertical fibres recovers the Néron–Tate pairing on the Jacobian $J(X_K)$ of dimension $g$.
\end{enumerate}
\item \emph{Genus correspondence.} $g$ is the single invariant entering (a) the Hodge numbers at infinity, (b) the rank of $\pi_*\omega_{X/S}$, (c) the dimension of the Arakelov Jacobian, (d) the archimedean $\Gamma$-factor $\Gamma_\mathbb{C}(s)^g$ of the Hasse–Weil $L$-function of $X_K$, (e) the coefficient in the arithmetic Noether formula $\widehat{\deg}\det R\pi_*\omega = \tfrac{1}{12}(\widehat{\omega}^2 + \delta_\mathrm{Ar})$ with $\deg \omega_{X_K/K} = 2g - 2$.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Theorem-waiting}. \emph{(Genus-$g$ arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row theorem.)} For the arithmetic Heisenberg attached to AAGF$_g$, with Satake parameters taking values in $GL_{2g}$ at each good prime and rank-$g$ Fock at each place, the Koszul self-duality
\[
\kappa^{\mathrm{Ar}}_{g, \mathsf{G}} + (\kappa^!)^{\mathrm{Ar}}_{g, \mathsf{G}} = 0
\]
holds if and only if the Hasse–Weil functional equation
\[
\Lambda_g(s, X_K) = \epsilon_{X_K}\,\Lambda_g(2 - s, X_K),\qquad \Lambda_g(s, X_K) := N_{X_K}^{s/2}\Gamma_\mathbb{C}(s)^g L(s, X_K),
\]
holds with $\epsilon_{X_K} \in \{\pm 1\}$ the Hasse–Weil root number. At $g = 0$ this degenerates to the Vol~IV $\mathsf{G}$-row theorem $\kappa^\mathrm{Ar} + (\kappa^!)^\mathrm{Ar} = 0 \Leftrightarrow \xi(s) = \xi(1 - s)$ for $X_K = \mathbb{P}^1$. The proof imports Weil's conjecture (Hasse 1934 for elliptic curves, Weil 1949 for abelian varieties, Faltings 1983 for the modularity-height input), Tate's thesis 1950 at genus $0$, and the arithmetic Heisenberg Koszul duality of Vol~IV, applied to the rank-$g$ Fock at each place.

This reading absorbs both (A) (genus 2 specifically: $L$-function functional equation with archimedean factor $\Gamma_\mathbb{C}(s)^2$) and (B) (arithmetic surface is always $2$-dim, cohomological signature at infinity is that of genus $g$ curve).

## Realization pointer

AAGF$_g$ sits in the existing Vol~IV arithmetic branch as a genus-graded family lying above Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:closure}. The $g = 0$ case is the Heisenberg Fock $\mathcal{V}^{\mathrm{prim}}_\mathrm{Heis}$ of the existing branch; Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:manifesto} axiomatises the base $\overline{C} = \overline{\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}}$, and the Hasse–Weil functional equation for $g = 0$ specialises to the Riemann functional equation $\xi(s) = \xi(1-s)$. AAGF$_g$ extends this by grading over $g$: the per-place rank-$g$ Fock, the Satake parameters in $GL_{2g}$, the archimedean factor $\Gamma_\mathbb{C}(s)^g$, and the arithmetic Noether formula of Faltings 1984 all scale by $g$.

AAGF$_g$ subsumes: (i) the existing Heisenberg Fock as $g = 0$; (ii) Faltings' $\delta$-invariant as the archimedean torsion entering the Noether formula; (iii) Bost–Künnemann's product formula as the formal statement of cohomological signature compatibility for self-products $X \times_\mathcal{O} X$. AAGF$_g$ extends: the programme's target from $\mathrm{GL}_2$ automorphic (which is $g = 1$, elliptic modular) to $\mathrm{GL}_{2g}$ automorphic for arbitrary $g$; the spectral side of F1 now takes values in $GL_{2g}$-parameters; the $E_2$-enhancement of F2 must carry rank-$g$ Fock structure per place.

AAGF$_g$ is orthogonal to: Deninger's $\mathbb{F}_1$-dynamical picture (explicitly out of scope, per Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:manifesto} \S~Not in Scope); the Caraiani–Scholze unitary Shimura variety programme (genus-$g$ arithmetic surfaces are not Shimura except in low genus); the Bethe-algebra heuristic window (primes as lattice sites) which lives at the zero-Fock per-place level and does not see $g$ directly.

The residual obligations for AAGF$_g$ are: (1) prove the genus-$g$ arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row theorem as Theorem-waiting (above), extending Vol~IV's current $g = 0$ load-bearing theorem; (2) supply the rank-$g$ arithmetic Positselski equivalence generalising the Iwasawa-Positselski machinery of Chapter~\ref{v4-arith:positselski}; (3) verify the Koszul–Petersson compatibility at rank $g$, replacing the scalar Petersson inner product with the genus-$g$ Petersson pairing on weight-$(k, g)$ automorphic forms; (4) extend F2 (perfectoid $E_2$-enhancement) to rank-$g$ local Iwahori Hecke categories via BD Grassmannians for $GL_{2g}/\mathbb{Q}_p$. Obligation (1) is the arithmetic avatar of the Weil conjecture for Jacobians; obligations (2)–(4) are routine extensions of the $g = 0$ Vol~IV machinery.

AAGF$_g$ does not introduce an almost complex structure in any non-integrable sense. The word ``almost'' is Falsified for arithmetic surfaces in reading (A); reading (B) is healed by replacing ``almost complex structure'' with ``canonical integrable complex structure on the archimedean fibre plus hermitian metric on $\widehat{\Omega}^1_{X/\mathbb{Z}}$''. The cohomological signature is two-layered: Hodge-Betti at infinity (captured by $g$) and arithmetic Chow (richer, encodes arithmetic intersection theory, Faltings–Hriljac Hodge index, height pairing). The genus correspondence is honest and central: $g$ is the single integer indexing the family, entering every local and global invariant of the arithmetic surface in the Arakelov sense.

The Arakelov voice converges with the other five voices of the swarm on this axiomatic AAGF$_g$ object and its genus-$g$ arithmetic $\mathsf{G}$-row theorem, provided each of the other voices accepts: (i) the integrable complex structure replaces any non-integrable almost complex structure; (ii) the arithmetic surface is $2$-dim as a scheme; (iii) the cohomological signature is two-layered and the ``genus-$g$'' identification is place-by-place at infinity plus arithmetic Chow globally; (iv) the Koszul self-duality functional equation at genus $g$ is the Hasse–Weil functional equation of the Jacobian, not a new functional equation invented for the occasion.
